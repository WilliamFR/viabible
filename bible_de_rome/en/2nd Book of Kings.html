2 Kings 1

1 Moab revolted against Israel after the death of Ahab. 2 Ahaziah fell out of the latticed window of his upper room in Samaria and became ill. He sent messengers and told them, "Go and consult Beel-Zebub, the god of Ekron, to find out if I will recover from this illness."« 3 But the angel of the Lord said to Elijah the Tishbite, «Get up, go up to meet the messengers of the king of Samaria and say to them, ‘Is it because there is no God in Israel that you are going to consult Beel-Zebub, the god of Acharon?’ 4 Therefore this is what the Lord says: «From the bed on which you have gone up you will not come down; you will certainly die.» And Elijah went away. 5 The messengers returned to Ahaziah and he said to them, "Why have you come back?"« 6 They answered him, «A man came up to meet us and said, »Go back to the king who sent you and tell him, “This is what the Lord says: Is it because there is no God in Israel that you are sending to consult Beel-Zebub, the god of Ekron? Therefore, from the bed you are lying on, you will not get down, but you will certainly die.”’” 7 Ahaziah asked them, "What was the appearance of the man who came up to meet you and spoke these words to you?"« 8 They answered him, "He was a hairy man with a leather belt around his waist." And Ahaziah said, "It is Elijah the Teshite."« 9 Immediately he sent to him a captain of fifty with his fifty men. This captain went up to Elijah and was sitting on the top of the mountain, and he said to him, «Man of God, the king has said: Come down.» 10 Elijah answered and said to the leader of fifty, «If I am a man of God, let fire come down from heaven and consume you and your fifty men?» And fire came down from heaven and consumed him and his fifty men. 11 Ahaziah sent yet another commander of fifty with his fifty men. This commander addressed Elijah, saying, "Man of God, this is what the king says: Hurry, go down."« 12 Elijah answered and said to them, «If I am a man of God, let fire come down from heaven and consume you and your fifty men.» And fire from God came down from heaven and consumed him and his fifty men. 13 Again Ahaziah sent a third commander of fifty with his fifty men. The third commander of fifty went up and, having arrived, he knelt before Elijah and said to him in supplication: «Man of God, please let my life and the lives of these fifty men, your servants, be precious in your sight. 14 "Look, fire came down from heaven and consumed the first two commanders of fifty and their fifty men, but now let my life be precious in your eyes."» 15 The angel of the Lord said to Elijah, «Go down with him and do not be afraid of him.» So Elijah got up and went down with him to the king. 16 He said to him, «This is what the Lord says: Because you have sent messengers to inquire of Beel-Zebub, the god of Ekron, is there no God in Israel whose word can be inquired of? Therefore, from the bed you are lying on you will not come down, but you will certainly die.» 17 Ahaziah died, according to the word of the Lord that Elijah had spoken, and Joram became king in his place in the second year of Joram son of Jehoshaphat, king of Judah, because he had no son. 18 The rest of the acts of Ahaziah, what he did, is it not written in the book of the Chronicles of the Kings of Israel?

    2 Kings 2

    1 When the Lord took Elijah up to heaven in a whirlwind, Elijah was leaving Gilgal with Elisha. 2 Elijah said to Elisha, «Please stay here, for the Lord has sent me to Bethel.» Elisha replied, «As surely as the Lord lives and as you live, I will not leave you.» So they went down to Bethel. 3 The sons of the prophets who were at Bethel went out to Elisha and said to him, «Do you know that the Lord will take your master from you today?» He replied, «Yes, I know; be at peace.» 4 Elijah said to him, «Elisha, please stay here, for the Lord has sent me to Jericho.» He replied, «As surely as the Lord lives and as you live, I will not leave you.» And they arrived at Jericho. 5 The sons of the prophets who were at Jericho approached Elisha and said to him, «Do you know that the Lord will take your master from you today?» He replied, «Yes, I know; be at peace.» 6 Elijah said to him, «Stay here, I beg you, for the Lord has sent me to the Jordan.» He replied, «As surely as the Lord lives and as you live, I will not leave you.» And they both went away. 7 Fifty men from among the sons of the prophets went after them and stood at a distance, on the opposite side, and the two of them stopped at the edge of the Jordan. 8 Then Elijah took his cloak, rolled it up, and struck the waters, and they parted to one side and to the other, and the two of them crossed over on dry ground. 9 When they had crossed over, Elijah said to Elisha, «Ask what I should do for you before I am taken from you.» Elisha replied, «May a double portion of your spirit come upon me?» 10 Elijah said, «You have asked a difficult thing. If you see me while I am being taken from you, it will be so for you; if not, it will not be so.» 11 They continued walking and talking together, and suddenly a chariot of fire and horses of fire separated them from each other, and Elijah went up to heaven in a whirlwind. 12 Elisha watched and cried out, «My father, my father, the chariot of Israel and its horsemen!» But he saw him no more. Then he took hold of his clothes and tore them in two pieces, 13 And he picked up Elijah's mantle, which had fallen off him. Then, returning to the Jordan, he stood on the bank, 14 And taking the mantle of Elijah that had fallen from him, he struck the waters with it and said, «Where is the Lord, the God of Elijah? Where is he?» When he had struck the waters, they parted to one side and to the other, and Elisha crossed over. 15 The sons of the prophets who were at Jericho, on the other side, saw him and said, "The spirit of Elijah rests on Elisha," and they went out to meet him and bowed down to the ground before him. 16 They said to him, «Look, there are fifty valiant men among your servants; let them go and seek your master. Perhaps the Spirit of the Lord has carried him away and thrown him down on one of the mountains or into one of the valleys.» He replied, «Do not send them.» 17 But they pressed him until he was embarrassed, and he said to them, "Send them." So they sent fifty men, who searched for Elijah for three days, but did not find him. 18 When they returned to Elisha, for he was staying in Jericho, he said to them, «Did I not tell you, »Do not go’?” 19 The people of the city said to Elisha, «The city is well situated, as my lord sees, but the waters are bad and the land is barren.» 20 He said, "Bring me a new bowl and put some salt in it." And they brought it to him. 21 He went to the spring of water and, having thrown salt into it, said, «Thus says the Lord: I have healed this water; from now on neither death nor barrenness will come from it.» 22 And the waters were purified to this day, according to the word that Elisha had spoken. 23 From there he went up to Bethel, and as he was going up the road, some little boys came out of the town and mocked him, saying, "Go up, bald man! Go up, bald man!"« 24 He turned to look at them and cursed them in the name of the Lord. And two bears came out of the forest and tore forty-two of those children to pieces. 25 From there he went to Mount Carmel and from there he returned to Samaria.

      2 Kings 3

      1 Joram, son of Ahab, became king of Israel in Samaria in the eighteenth year of Jehoshaphat, king of Judah, and he reigned twelve years. 2 He did what was evil in the sight of the Lord, but not like his father and mother, for he removed the pillar of Baal, which his father had made. 3 But he clung to the sins of Jeroboam, son of Nebat, who had caused Israel to sin, and he did not turn away from them. 4 Mesha, king of Moab, owned flocks and he paid the king of Israel a tribute of one hundred thousand lambs and one hundred thousand rams with their fleeces. 5 Upon Ahab's death, the king of Moab revolted against the king of Israel. 6 King Joram therefore went out that day from Samaria and surveyed all Israel. 7 Having set out, he sent word to Jehoshaphat, king of Judah: «The king of Moab has rebelled against me; will you come with me to attack Moab?» Jehoshaphat replied: «I will go up; it will be to me as to you, to my people as to your people, to my horses as to your horses.» 8 And he said, «By which route shall we go up?» Joram replied, «By the way of the desert of Edom.» 9 The king of Israel, the king of Judah, and the king of Edom set out. After a seven-day march, there was no water for the army or for the animals that followed it. 10 Then the king of Israel said, «Alas! The Lord has called these three kings to deliver them into the hands of Moab.» 11 But Jehoshaphat said, «Is there no prophet of the Lord here, through whom we may inquire of the Lord?» One of the servants of the king of Israel answered and said, «Elisha, the son of Shaphat, is here; he used to pour water on Elijah’s hands.» 12 And Jehoshaphat said, "The word of the Lord is with him." The king of Israel, Jehoshaphat, king of Judah, and the king of Edom went down to him. 13 Elisha said to the king of Israel, «What do you want with me? Go to the prophets of your father and to the prophets of your mother.» And the king of Israel said to him, «No, for the Lord has called these three kings to deliver them into the hand of Moab.» 14 Elisha said, «As surely as the Lord Almighty lives, before whom I stand, if I did not respect Jehoshaphat king of Judah, I would not pay any attention to you or even look at you.”. 15 "Now bring me a harpist." And while the harpist was playing, the hand of the Lord was upon Elisha, 16 And he said, «Thus says the Lord: Make in this valley pits and pits. 17 For thus says the Lord: You will see no wind and you will see no rain, and this valley will be filled with water, and you, your flocks and your beasts of burden will drink. 18 But this is a small thing in the eyes of the Lord: he will deliver Moab into your hands. 19 You will attack all the fortified cities and all the important cities, you will cut down all the fruit trees, you will block up all the springs of water, and you will devastate all the best fields by covering them with stones.» 20 In fact, in the morning, at the time when the offering was made, behold, water came by way of Edom, and the land was filled with water. 21 However, when all the Moabites learned that the kings were coming up to attack them, all the men capable of wearing a baldric and even more were summoned and they took up positions on the border. 22 They rose early in the morning and, when the sun shone on the waters, the Moabites saw before them the waters red like blood. 23 They said, "This is bloodshed. The kings are destroyed; they have struck one another, and now the Moabites are plundered."« 24 And they advanced toward the camp of Israel. But the Israelites rose up and attacked Moab, who fled before them. Entering the land, they attacked Moab, 25 They destroyed the cities, each throwing their stone into all the best fields, they filled them with it, they blocked all the springs of water, they cut down all the fruit trees, to the point that of Qir-Charoseth only the stones remained, the slingers had surrounded and beaten it. 26 When the king of Moab saw that he was losing the battle, he took with him seven hundred men, with drawn swords, to fight his way to the king of Edom, but they could not succeed. 27 Then he took his firstborn son, who was to reign in his place, and offered him as a burnt offering on the wall. And great indignation seized Israel, and they withdrew from the king of Moab and returned to their own land.

        2 Kings 4

        1 A woman, one of the wives of the sons of the prophets, cried out to Elisha, saying, «Your servant, my husband, is dead, and you know that your servant feared the Lord. Now the creditor has come and taken my two children to be his slaves.» 2 Elisha said to her, «What can I do for you? Tell me, what do you have in the house?» She replied, «Your servant has nothing at all in the house except a jar of oil.» 3 He said, «Go outside and ask all your neighbors for empty vessels, and not just a few. 4 When you get home, shut the door behind you and your children, pour some of your oil into all these jars, and set aside those that are full.» 5 So she left him. She closed the door on herself and her children; they brought the vases to her and she poured. 6 When the jars were full, she said to her son, "Bring me another jar." But he replied, "There is no more jar." And the oil stopped flowing. 7 She went and told the man of God, and he said, «Go, sell the oil and pay your debt, and you and your sons can live on what remains.» 8 One day Elisha was passing through Shunem. There was a rich woman there, who urged him to accept a meal, and every time he passed by, he went to her house to eat. 9 She said to her husband, "Look, I know that he is a holy man of God, who often comes to our house like this. 10 Let's make him a small, high room against the wall and put in it a bed, a table, a chair, and a candlestick, so that he can retire there when he comes to our house.» 11 Elisha, having returned another day to Shunem, retired to the upper room and slept there. 12 He said to Giezi, his servant, "Call this Shunammite woman." Giezi called her, and she came before him. 13 And Elisha said to Giez, «Tell her, »You have shown us all this concern: what can be done for you? Should we speak to the king or the commander of the army on your behalf?«» She answered Giez, “I live among my own people.” 14 And Elisha said, "What can be done for her?" Giezi replied, "But she has no son, and her husband is old."« 15 And Elisha said, "Call her." Giezi called her, and she stood at the door. 16 And Elisha said to her, «At this time next year, you will have a son.» And she said, «No, my lord, man of God, do not deceive your servant.» 17 And the woman conceived and gave birth to a son at the same time the following year, just as Elisha had told her. 18 The child grew up. One day, when he had gone to find his father among the harvesters, 19 He said to his father, "My head! My head!" The father said to his servant, "Take him to his mother."« 20 The servant having taken him away and brought him to his mother, the child remained on his mother's lap until noon, then he died. 21 She went upstairs, laid him on the bed of the man of God, closed the door on him and left. 22 She called her husband and said, "Please send me one of the servants and one of the donkeys, so that I may run to the man of God and return immediately.". 23 He said, "Why go to him today? It is neither the new moon nor the Sabbath." She replied, "Don't worry."« 24 And, having saddled the donkey, she said to her servant, "Lead me on and go, do not stop me on the way unless I tell you to."« 25 She left and went to the man of God on Mount Carmel. When the man of God saw her from afar, he said to Giezhi, his servant, «Here is the Shunammite woman. 26 So run to meet her and say to her, »Are you well? Is your husband well? Is your child well?« She replied, »Well.” 27 As soon as she arrived at the man of God's place on the mountain, she seized his feet. Giezi approached to push her away, but the man of God said, "Leave her alone, for her soul is in bitterness, and the Lord has hidden it from me and has not revealed it to me."« 28 Then she said, "Did I ask my lord for a son? Did I not say, 'Do not deceive me'?"« 29 And Elisha said to Giez, «Gird up your loins, take my staff in your hand and go. If you meet anyone, do not greet him, and if anyone greets you, do not answer him. You shall lay my staff on the face of the child.» 30 The child's mother said, "As the Lord lives and as your soul lives, I will not leave you." And Elisha got up and followed her. 31 Giezi had gone ahead and placed the staff on the child's face, but there was no sound, no sign of attention. He returned to meet Elisha and reported the matter, saying, "The child has not woken up."« 32 When Elisha arrived at the house, behold, the child was dead, lying on his bed. 33 Elisha went in and, having closed the door behind them both, he prayed to the Lord. 34 And he went up and lay on the child, he put his mouth on his mouth, his eyes on his eyes, his hands on his hands, and he stretched himself out on him, and the child's flesh grew warm. 35 Elisha went away and walked to and fro in the house, then he climbed back onto the bed and lay down on the child and the child sneezed seven times and the child opened his eyes. 36 Elisha called Giez and said, "Call the Shunammite woman." Giez called her, and she came to Elisha, who said to her, "Take your son."« 37 She came and fell at his feet and prostrated herself on the ground and, taking her son, she went out. 38 Elisha returned to Gilgal, and the famine was in the land. As the sons of the prophets were sitting before him, he said to his servant, «Put on the large pot and cook some stew for the sons of the prophets.» 39 One of them went out into the fields to gather herbs, found a kind of wild vine, and picked wild gourds, filling his garment. When he returned, he cut them into pieces and added them to the pot where the soup was being prepared, without their knowledge. 40 They poured soup for these men to eat, but as soon as they had eaten the soup, they cried out, saying, "Death is in the pot, man of God!" And they could not eat. 41 Elisha said, "Bring me some flour." He threw some into the jar and said, "Pour it out to the people so they may eat." And there was nothing bad left in the jar. 42 A man came from Baal-Salisah, bringing to the man of God some bread from the firstfruits, namely twenty barley loaves and some fresh wheat in his sack. Elisha said, «Give it to the people so they may eat.» 43 His servant replied, "How can I serve this in front of a hundred people?" But Elisha said, "Give it to the people so they can eat. For this is what the Lord says: 'They will eat and there will be some left over.'"« 44 So he placed the loaves before them and they ate and had some left over, according to the word of the Lord.

          2 Kings 5

          1 Naaman, commander of the army of the king of Syria, was a powerful and respected man in the sight of his master, for it was through him that the Lord had granted salvation to the Syrians, but this strong and valiant man was a leper. 2 But the Syrians, having gone out in bands, had taken captive a little girl from the land of Israel, who was in the service of Naaman's wife. 3 She said to her mistress, "Oh. If my lord were with the prophet who is in Samaria, the prophet would deliver him from his leprosy."« 4 Naaman came and reported this to his master, saying, "The young woman from the land of Israel spoke in such and such a way."« 5 And the king of Syria said, «Go, and I will send a letter to the king of Israel.» So he went, taking with him ten talents of silver, six thousand shekels of gold, and ten changes of clothing. 6 He brought to the king of Israel the letter which read: «Now therefore, when this letter reaches you, behold, I am sending you Naaman, my servant, so that you may cure him of his leprosy.» 7 After reading the letter, the king of Israel tore his clothes and said, «Am I a god, able to kill and to give life, that he should send me to cure a man of his leprosy? Know then and see that he is seeking a quarrel with me.» 8 When Elisha, the man of God, heard that the king of Israel had torn his clothes, he sent word to the king: «Why have you torn your clothes? Let him come to me and he will know that there is a prophet in Israel.» 9 Naaman came with his horses and chariot and stopped at the door of Elisha's house. 10 Elisha sent a messenger to him, saying, «Go and wash yourself seven times in the Jordan, and your flesh will be restored and you will be clean.» 11 Naaman was angry and went away, saying, «I thought he would surely come out to me and stand and call on the name of the Lord his God, and wave his hand over the sore and heal the leper. 12 Are not the Abana and Pharphar rivers of Damascus better than all the waters of Israel? Could I not wash in them and become clean?» And turning away, he went off in anger. 13 His servants approached him to speak to him and said, «My father, if the prophet had asked you to do something difficult, would you not have done it? How much more should you obey him when he tells you, »Wash and be clean’?” 14 He went down and dipped himself seven times in the Jordan, according to the word of the man of God, and his flesh became like the flesh of a little child and he was cleansed. 15 Naaman returned to the man of God with all his entourage. When he arrived, he stood before him and said, «Now I know that there is no God in all the earth except in Israel. So now accept a gift from your servant.» 16 Elisha replied, "As surely as the Lord lives before whom I stand, I will not accept it." Naaman urged him to accept, but he refused. 17 And Naaman said, «Otherwise, allow your servant to be given two mules’ loads of earth, for your servant will no longer offer burnt offerings or sacrifices to any other gods except the Lord. 18 However, may the Lord forgive your servant this: When my master enters the house of Remmon to worship and leans on my hand, and I also bow down in the house of Remmon, may the Lord forgive your servant if I bow down in the house of Remmon.» 19 Elisha said to him, «Go in peace.» And Naaman left Elisha. He was some distance away, 20 When Gizi, the servant of Elisha, the man of God, said to himself, «Look, my master has spared Naaman, this Syrian, by not accepting from his hand what he brought. As the Lord lives, I will run after him and get something from him.» 21 And Giezi began to pursue Naaman. When Naaman saw him running after him, he got down from his chariot to meet him and said, "Is everything all right?"« 22 Giezi replied, «All is well. My master sends me to tell you: Two young men from the hill country of Ephraim, descendants of the prophets, have just arrived at my house. Please give them a talent of silver and two changes of clothing.» 23 Naaman said, "Agree to take two talents." He urged him to accept and, having packed two talents of silver in two bags with two changes of clothes, he gave them to two of his servants to carry before Giezi. 24 Arriving at the hill, Giézi took them from their hands and placed them in the house, then he sent the men away, and they left. 25 And he went and presented himself to his master. Elisha said to him, «Where have you been, Giez?» He replied, «Your servant has gone neither to one side nor the other.» 26 But Elisha said to him, «Did not my spirit go with you when the man left his chariot to meet you? Is this the time to accept money and to accept clothing and olive trees and vineyards and sheep and oxen and male and female servants? 27 "Naaman's leprosy will cling to you and your descendants forever." And Giezi went out from Elisha's presence with leprosy as white as snow.

          2 Kings 6

          1 The sons of the prophets said to Elisha, «Look, the place where we are sitting before you is too small for us. 2 "Let's go to the Jordan, and each of us will take a beam and make ourselves a place to live in." Elisha replied, "Go."» 3 And one of them said, "Agree to come with your servants." He replied, "I will go.", 4 And he went with them. When they arrived at the Jordan, they cut wood. 5 As one of them was cutting down a beam, the iron fell into the water; he cried out and said, "Alas, my lord, and I had borrowed it."« 6 The man of God said, "Where did it fall?" And he showed him the place. Then Elisha cut a piece of wood, threw it into that spot, and the iron floated to the surface. 7 And he said, "Take it." He reached out his hand and took it. 8 The king of Syria was at war with Israel. After consulting with his servants, he said, "My camp will be in such and such a place."« 9 But the man of God sent word to the king of Israel: «Beware of crossing into that place, for the Syrians are coming down there.» 10 And the king of Israel sent people to the place that the man of God had told him about and pointed out to him, and he was on his guard there more than once. 11 The heart of the king of Syria was troubled by this maneuver; he summoned his servants and said to them, "Will you not tell me which of us is for the king of Israel?"« 12 One of his servants replied, "No one, O king, my lord, but Elisha the prophet, who is in Israel, reports to the king of Israel the words that you speak in your bedroom."« 13 The king said, "Go and see where he is, and I will send for him." They came and told him, "He is in Dothan."« 14 So he sent horses, chariots and a large troop, which arrived one night and surrounded the city. 15 The servant of the man of God got up early in the morning and went out, and behold, an army with horses and chariots surrounded the city. And the servant said to Elisha, «Ah, my lord, what shall we do?» 16 He replied, "Do not be afraid, for those who are with us are more than those who are with them."« 17 Elisha prayed and said, «Lord, open his eyes so that he may see.» And the Lord opened the servant’s eyes, and he saw, and behold, the mountain was full of horses and chariots of fire all around Elisha. 18 The Syrians went down to the man of God. Elisha prayed to the Lord and said, «Please strike this nation with blindness.» And the Lord struck them with blindness, according to the word of Elisha. 19 Elisha said to them, «This is not the way and this is not the city; follow me and I will lead you to the man you are looking for.» And he led them to Samaria. 20 When they had entered Samaria, Elisha said, «Lord, open the eyes of these people so that they may see.» And the Lord opened their eyes, and they saw, and behold, they were in the midst of Samaria. 21 When the king of Israel saw them, he said to Elisha, «Shall I strike them down, shall I strike them down, my father?» 22 And Elisha replied, «You shall not strike them. Those whom you have taken captive with your sword and bow, strike them, but place bread and water before these men, so that they may eat and drink and then go to their master.» 23 The king of Israel prepared a great feast for them, and they ate and drank. Then he sent them away, and they returned to their master. And the Syrian bands never returned to the territory of Israel. 24 After that, Benhadad, king of Syria, having gathered all his army, went up and besieged Samaria. 25 There was a great famine in Samaria and it was besieged so severely that a donkey's head was worth eighty shekels of silver and a quarter of a measure of pigeon dung was worth five shekels of silver. 26 As the king was passing over the wall, a woman cried out to him, saying, "Save me, O king, my lord."« 27 He said, «If the Lord does not save you, how can I save you? From the produce of the threshing floor or the winepress?» 28 And the king said to her, "What is the matter with you?"                             2 Kings 15

                                2 Kings 17

                                1 In the twelfth year of Ahaz king of Judah, Hoshea son of Elah reigned over Israel in Samaria, and his reign lasted nine years. 2 He did what was evil in the sight of the Lord, but not like the kings of Israel who had been before him. 3 Shalmaneser, king of Assyria, came up against him and Hoshea was subjugated and paid him tribute. 4 But the king of Assyria discovered a conspiracy by Hoshea, who had sent messengers to Sua, king of Egypt, and who was no longer paying tribute to the king of Assyria, year after year, so the king of Assyria had him seized and thrown in chains into a prison. 5 And the king of Assyria traveled throughout the land and came up against Samaria, besieging it for three years. 6 In the ninth year of Hosea, the king of Assyria captured Samaria and carried Israel captive to Assyria. He assigned them Halah, the banks of the Habor, the river of Gosan, and the cities of the Medes as their dwelling place. 7 This happened because the children of Israel had sinned against the Lord their God, who had brought them up out of the land of Egypt, from under the hand of Pharaoh, king of Egypt, and because they had feared other gods. 8 They followed the rites of the nations that the Lord had driven out before the children of Israel and those that the kings of Israel had established. 9 The children of Israel disguised under a false appearance things that were not right in the sight of the Lord their God. They built themselves holy places in all their cities, from watchtowers to fortified cities. 10 They set up pillars and Asherahs on every high hill and under every green tree. 11 And there they burned incense on all the holy places, like the nations that the Lord had taken captive before them, and they did evil things, thus provoking the Lord. 12 They served idols, about which the Lord had told them, «You shall not do that.» 13 The Lord bore witness against Israel and Judah through all his prophets and seers, saying, «Turn from your evil ways and keep my commandments and my ordinances, following all the law that I commanded your fathers and that I sent to you by my servants the prophets.» 14 But they did not listen and they stiffened their necks, like their fathers, who did not believe in the Lord their God. 15 They rejected his ordinances and the covenant he had made with their ancestors, and the testimonies he had given against them. They went after worthless things and indulged in vanity, following the nations around them, whom the Lord had commanded them not to imitate. 16 They abandoned all the commandments of the Lord their God, they made for themselves two molten calves and they made themselves Asherahs, they bowed down before all the host of heaven and they served Baal. 17 They made their sons and daughters pass through the fire, they practiced divination and enchantments, and they devoted themselves to doing evil in the sight of the Lord, provoking him to anger. 18 And the Lord was very angry with Israel and removed them from his presence. And Israel was taken captive from their land to Assyria, where they remain to this day. 24 The king of Assyria brought people from Babylon, Cuthah, Avah, Emath, and Sepharvaim and settled them in the cities of Samaria in place of the children of Israel; they took possession of Samaria and lived in its cities. 25 When they began to live there, they did not fear the Lord, and the Lord sent lions against them, and they killed them. 26 So this report was made to the king of Assyria: «The nations you have deported and settled in the cities of Samaria do not know how to serve the god of the land, and he has sent lions against them, and behold, they are killing them because they do not know how to serve the god of the land.» 27 The king of Assyria gave this order: «Send one of the priests you brought from there into captivity, let him go and settle there and teach them how to serve the god of the land.» 28 One of the priests who had been taken captive from Samaria came to settle in Bethel and taught them how they should honor the Lord. 29 But each nation made its own gods and placed them in the houses of the sacred places built by the Samaritans, each nation in the city it inhabited. 30 The people of Babylon made Sochoth-Benoth, the people of Cuthah made Nergel, the people of Emath made Asima, 31 Those of Avah made Nebahaz and Tarthach, and those of Shapharvaim gave their children to the fire in honor of Adramelech and Anamelech, gods of Shapharvaim. 32 They also honored the Lord and appointed priests of the holy places from among all the people, and these priests offered sacrifices for them in the houses of the holy places. 33 Thus they honored the Lord and at the same time served their gods according to the custom of the nations from which they had been deported. 34 They follow the old customs to this day;                                   2 Kings 18

he burned down all the great houses. 10 The entire Chaldean army, which was with the captain of the guards, demolished the walls forming the enclosure of Jerusalem. 11 Nebuzardan, captain of the guards, took captive the rest of the people who had remained in the city, the deserters who had surrendered to the king of Babylon, and the rest of the multitude. 12 The captain of the guards left some of the poor of the country as winegrowers and farmers. 13 The Chaldeans broke down the bronze pillars that were in the house of the Lord, as well as the bases and the bronze sea that were in the house of the Lord, and they carried the bronze away to Babylon. 14 They took the pots, shovels, knives, cups and all the bronze utensils used for serving. 15 The captain of the guards also took the censers and cups, both those made of gold and those made of silver. 16 As for the two pillars, the sea, and the bases that Solomon had made in the house of the Lord, there was no need to weigh the bronze of all these utensils. 17 The height of one column was eighteen cubits and there was on top of it a bronze capital and the height of the capital was three cubits and there was all around the capital a trellis and pomegranates, all of bronze, it was the same of the second column with the trellis. 18 The captain of the guards took Saraias, the high priest, Zephaniah, a priest of the second rank, and the three gatekeepers. 19 In the city, he took an officer who commanded the soldiers, five men who were part of the king's privy council and who were found in the city, the secretary of the army commander in charge of enlisting the people of the country, and sixty men from the people of the country who were in the city. 20 Having captured them, Nebuzardan, captain of the guards, led them to the king of Babylon at Rebla. 21 And the king of Babylon struck them down and put them to death at Rebla in the land of Emath. So Judah was taken captive from its land. 22 As for the people who remained in the land of Judah whom Nebuchadnezzar, king of Babylon, left there, he gave to them as governor Gedaliah, son of Ahicam, son of Shaphan. 23 When all the commanders of the troops had learned, they and their men, that the king of Babylon had appointed Gedaliah as governor, they came to Gedaliah at Maspha, namely: Ishmael son of Nathaniah, Johanan son of Karea, Saraiah son of Thanehumeth of Netophah and Jezoniah son of the Maachathite, they and their men. 24 Godolias swore to them and their men, saying, «Do not fear the servants of the Chaldeans; remain in the land, serve the king of Babylon, and all will be well with you.» 25 But in the seventh month, Ishmael, son of Nathanias, son of Elishama, of the royal line, came with ten men and they struck down Gedaliah, as well as the Jews and Chaldeans who were with him at Maspha. 26 Then all the people, from the least to the greatest, and the commanders of the troops, arose and went to Egypt, because they were afraid of the Chaldeans. 27 In the thirty-seventh year of the captivity of Jehoiachin king of Judah, in the twelfth month, on the twenty-seventh day of the month, Evil-Merodach king of Babylon, in the year of his accession, lifted up the head of Jehoiachin king of Judah and brought him out of prison. 28 He spoke kindly to him and placed his throne above the thrones of the kings who were with him in Babylon. 29 He made him change his prison clothes and Joachim always ate in his presence, all the rest of his life. 30 As for its upkeep, its perpetual upkeep, the king provided for it every day, throughout his entire life.
