1 Maccabees 1

1 When Alexander, son of Philip, a Macedonian, came from the land of Cetim, defeated Darius, king of the Persians and Medes, and became king in his place, after having first reigned over Greece, 2 He waged many wars, captured many fortresses, and put to death kings of the earth. 3 He went to the ends of the earth and seized the spoils of many nations, and the earth was silent before him. 4 His heart was lifted up and swelled with pride; he gathered a very strong army. 5 and subdued lands, nations, and rulers, and they became his tributaries. 6 After that, he fell onto his bed and knew he was going to die. 7 He summoned his higher-ranking officers, the companions of his youth, and divided his empire among them while he was still alive. 8 Alexander reigned for twelve years and then he died. 9 His officers took possession of power, each in his own place. 10 They all wore the diadem after his death, and their sons after them, for many years, and they multiplied evils on the earth. 11 From these kings came a root of iniquity, Antiochus Epiphanes, son of King Antiochus, who had been in Rome as a hostage, and he became king in the one hundred and thirty-seventh year of the kingdom of the Greeks. 12 In those days, unbelieving children went out from Israel, leading many others astray, saying, «Let us go and join the nations around us, for since we separated ourselves from them, many calamities have befallen us.» 13 And this speech seemed good to them. 14 Some of the people hurried to the king and he gave them permission to follow the customs of the nations. 15 So they built a gymnasium in Jerusalem, according to the customs of the nations. 16 They removed the marks of their circumcision and thus, separating themselves from the holy covenant, they allied themselves with the nations and sold themselves to do sin. 17 When his power seemed firmly established, Antiochus considered ruling over Egypt, in order to be sovereign of both kingdoms. 18 He entered Egypt with a powerful army, with chariots, elephants and horsemen and a large number of ships. 19 He attacked Ptolemy, king of Egypt, but Ptolemy was afraid of him and fled, and a multitude of men fell, struck down and killed. 20 The Syrians took the fortified cities of the land of Egypt and Antiochus carried off the spoils of all Egypt. 21 After defeating Egypt in the year 143, Antiochus retraced his steps and marched against Israel. 22 Having marched to Jerusalem with a powerful army, 23 He entered with insolent audacity into the sanctuary and removed the golden altar, the lampstand of light with all its utensils, the table of the showbread, the golden cups, bowls and dishes, the curtain, the crowns and the golden ornaments on the front of the temple, and he detached the plating everywhere. 24 He also took the gold and silver and the precious vessels, as well as any hidden treasures he could find. Carrying everything, he returned to his own country., 25 after massacring many people and uttering insolent words. 26 There was great mourning among the Israelites in all the places where they lived. 27 The chiefs and elders groaned, the young girls and young men lost their vigor, and the beauty of the women deteriorated. 28 The new husband made lamentations, and sitting in the bridal chamber, the young bride shed tears. 29 The earth trembled for its inhabitants, and all of Jacob's family was covered in confusion. 30 Two years later, the king sent a tax collector to the cities of Judah. He arrived in Jerusalem with a large number of troops. 31 And he cunningly addressed friendly words to the inhabitants, who welcomed him without suspicion., 32 Then, all of a sudden, he rushed upon the city, struck it with a great plague, and killed many Israelites. 33 He looted the city, set it on fire, tore down houses and demolished the city walls. 34 He took the women and children captive and seized the livestock. 35 Then the Syrians surrounded the city of David with a great and strong wall, with powerful towers: this was their citadel. 36 They placed a perverse race there, people without faith or law, and they strengthened themselves there. 37 They piled up weapons and provisions there and, gathering the spoils of Jerusalem, they deposited them there, thus becoming a great danger to the city. 38 This citadel was like an ambush set against the sanctuary and a formidable adversary for Israel throughout this time, 39 They also spilled innocent blood around the temple and defiled the sanctuary. 40 Because of them, the inhabitants fled from Jerusalem, which became a place of foreigners. The city became foreign to those who were born there; its own children had abandoned it. 41 Her sanctuary remained desolate like a desert, her festivals turned into days of mourning, her Sabbaths into disgrace, and what had been her honor became a cause of outrage. 42 His humiliation increased in proportion to his glory, and his greatness turned into mourning. 43 King Antiochus issued an edict throughout his kingdom, so that all would become one people and each would abandon their particular law. 44 All nations complied with the king's order. 45 Many Israelites also consented to follow his worship; they sacrificed to idols and profaned the Sabbath. 46 The king sent letters by messengers to Jerusalem and the other cities of Judah, ordering them to follow the customs of foreigners in the land, 47 to put an end to burnt offerings, sacrifices, and libations in the temple, 48 to profane the Sabbaths and the feasts, 49 to defile the sanctuary and the saints, 50 to build altars, sacred groves and idol temples, and to offer pigs and other unclean animals as sacrifices, 51 to leave their male children uncircumcised, to defile themselves with all kinds of impurities and profanations, so as to make them forget the law and change all its prescriptions. 52 And anyone who did not obey the orders of King Antiochus would be punished by death. 53 These are the letters he published throughout his kingdom, and he appointed overseers over all the people., 54 He also commanded the cities of Judah to offer sacrifices in each city. 55 Many Jews, all those who abandoned the law, sided with the Syrians and practiced evil in the land., 56and forced the faithful Israelites to take refuge in hiding places, in all kinds of retreats. 57 On the fifteenth day of the month of Casleh, in the year one hundred and forty-five, they erected the abomination of desolation on the altar of burnt offering. They also erected altars in the surrounding towns of Judah. 58 They burned incense at the doors of houses and in the squares. 59 If they found the books of the law anywhere, they tore them up and burned them. 60 Anyone in whose possession a book of the covenant was found, and whoever showed adherence to the law, was put to death by the king's edict. 61 It was with this violence that they treated Israel, executing in the cities, one day each month, those who were caught in violation. 62 On the twenty-fifth of the month, they offered a sacrifice on the altar that had been built on the altar of burnt offerings. 63 According to the edict, women who had their children circumcised were also put to death., 64 by hanging the children around their necks, they looted their houses and killed those who had carried out the operation. 65 However, many Israelites bravely resisted and made a firm resolution not to eat anything unclean. They preferred to die rather than defile themselves with food. 66 and to profane the holy covenant, and they died. 67 It was a very great wrath that was being unleashed upon Israel.

1 Maccabees 2

1 In those days there appeared Mattathias, son of John, son of Simeon, priest of the sons of Joarib of Jerusalem, who lived in Modin. 2 He had five sons: John, nicknamed Gaddis, 3 Simon, called Thasi, 4 Judas, nicknamed Maccabeus, 5 Eleazar, nicknamed Abaron, and Jonathan, nicknamed Apphus. 6 Seeing the outrages that were being committed in Judah and Jerusalem, 7 Mathathias said, «Alas, why was I born to see the ruin of my people and the ruin of the holy city, and to remain here idle while it is delivered into the hands of the enemy?” 8 And that its sanctuary is in the power of foreigners? Its temple has become like the dwelling place of a vile man, 9 The precious objects that were its glory were carried off like spoils, its grandchildren were massacred in its streets, the enemy's sword felled its young men. 10 What people did not inherit their kingdom and receive their share of its spoils? 11 They took away all her finery; from free, she became a slave. 12 Everything we had that was holy, beautiful, and glorious has been ravaged and desecrated by the nations. 13Why should we still be alive?» 14 Then Mattathias and his sons tore their clothes, covered themselves with sackcloth, and mourned deeply. 15 The king's officers tasked with forcing apostasy came to Modin to organize sacrifices. 16 A large number of Israelites joined them, and Mattathias and his sons also gathered on their side. 17 Antiochus' envoys, addressing Mathathias, said to him: "You are the first in this city, the greatest in regard and influence, and surrounded by sons and brothers. 18 "Go first and carry out the king's command, as all the nations have done, the men of Judah and those who remained in Jerusalem, and you and your family will be among the king's friends, you and your sons, and you will receive ornaments of gold and silver and many gifts."» 19 Mathathias replied and said aloud: «Even if all the nations that are part of Antiochus’ kingdom were to obey him, each abandoning the worship of their fathers and willingly submitting to his commands, 20 I, my sons and my brothers, will follow the covenant of our fathers. 21 May God keep us from abandoning the law and its precepts. 22 We will not obey the king's orders to deviate from our faith, whether to the right or to the left.» 23 As soon as he had finished this speech, a Jew stepped forward in full view of everyone to sacrifice, according to the king's order, on the altar erected at Modin. 24 At this sight, Mathathias was indignant and shuddered to the depths of his being; he let his anger rise according to the law and, rushing forward, he killed this man on the altar. 25He simultaneously killed the king's officer who was forcing a sacrifice and overturned the altar. 26 Thus he was filled with zeal for the law, like Phinehas, who killed Zambri, son of Salum. 27 Then Mattathias went through the city, shouting aloud, «Whoever is zealous for the law and upholds the covenant, let him leave the city and follow me.» 28 And he and his sons fled to the mountains, abandoning everything they owned in the city. 29 A large number of Jews, seeking justice and the law, then went down into the desert, 30 to remain there, they, their children and their wives, as well as their livestock, because the evils that afflicted them had reached their peak. 31 The king's officers and troops in Jerusalem, in the city of David, were informed that some men who had disobeyed the king's order had gone down into the desert to hidden retreats. 32 Immediately a large number of soldiers set out in pursuit of them. When they caught up with them, they camped opposite them and prepared to attack them on the Sabbath day. 33 They told them, "It's enough that you've resisted until now. Go out and carry out the king's order, and you will live."« 34 The Jews replied, "We will not go out and we will not obey the king's order; that would be violating the Sabbath day."« 35 The Syrians immediately engaged them in combat. 36 They did not answer them, did not throw a single stone at them, and did not block their retreat. 37 «Let us all die,» they said, “in the simplicity of our hearts. Heaven and earth bear witness for us that you are making us die unjustly.” 38 The soldiers attacked them on the Sabbath day, and they, their wives and children, as well as their livestock, died; there were about a thousand men. 39 Mathathias and his friends learned of this massacre and they felt great sorrow. 40 And they said to one another, "If we all do as our brothers have done and do not fight against the nations for our lives and for our institutions, they will soon have exterminated us from the earth."« 41 So they made this resolution that day: «Whoever comes to war against us on the Sabbath day, let us fight him and not allow ourselves to be killed as our brothers did in their retreat.» 42 Then a band of Assidenes joined them, made up of valiant men of Israel, from all those whose hearts were attached to the law. 43 All those who were seeking to escape the present evils also came to them and increased their strength. 44 Having thus formed an army, they first struck the wrongdoers in their anger and the wicked in their indignation, the rest sought salvation by fleeing to the nations. 45 Mattathias and his sons traveled throughout the land; they destroyed the altars, 46 They forcibly circumcised all the uncircumcised children they found in the land of Israel, 47 and pursued those puffed up with pride. The enterprise succeeded under their leadership, 48 They upheld the cause of the law against the power of the pagans and against the power of kings, and they did not bow down before the sinner. 49 When the days of Mathathias came to an end, he said to his sons: «Now pride reigns and punishment prevails; it is a time of ruin and fierce anger. 50 Therefore, O my sons, be zealous for the law and give your lives for the covenant of our fathers. 51 Remember the works that our fathers accomplished in their time and you will receive glory and an immortal name. 52 Was not Abraham found faithful in the test, and was not his faith credited to him as righteousness? 53 Joseph, in the time of his affliction, kept the commandments and he became lord of Egypt. 54 Phinehas, our father, because he burned with zeal for the cause of God, received the assurance of a holy priesthood. 55 Jesus, having fulfilled the word, became a judge in Israel. 56 Caleb, for having borne witness in the assembly, received a portion of the land. 57 David, through his piety, obtained a royal throne for all ages. 58 Elijah, because he was very zealous for the law, was taken up to heaven. 59Ananias, Azarias and Misael, having trusted, were saved from the flames. 60 Daniel, through his innocence, was delivered from the mouths of the lions. 61 Therefore, consider that in all ages, all who hope in Him do not perish. 62 Do not fear the threats of a sinful man, for his glory goes to corruption and worms. 63 He rises today and tomorrow he will no longer be found, because he will have returned to his dust and his thoughts will have vanished. 64 Therefore, my sons, be strong and valiant in defending the law, for by it you will be glorified. 65 This is Simon, your brother; I know that he is a man of counsel, always listen to him, he will be a father to you. 66 Let Judas Maccabeus, a valiant hero since his youth, be the leader of your army and direct the war against the peoples. 67 You will enlist all the observers of the law and you will avenge your people. 68 "Render unto the nations what they have done unto Israel, and observe the commandments of the law."» 69 And after he had blessed them, he was gathered to his fathers. 70 He died in the year one hundred and forty-six, his sons buried him in the tomb of their fathers at Modin and Israel mourned him in great grief.

1 Maccabees 3

1 Judas, his son, nicknamed Maccabeus, rose up after him. 2 He had as helpers all his brothers and all those who had joined his father, and together they joyfully fought the battles of Israel. 3 He spread the glory of his people far and wide, he donned the armor like a hero, he girded on his weapons of war and engaged in battles, protecting the camp of Israel with his sword. 4 In action, he was like a lion, like a lion cub roaring at its prey. 5 He pursued the wicked, searching their hideouts and delivered to the flames those who troubled his people. 6 The wicked retreated in fear before him, all the workers of iniquity were in terror, and his hand happily led the deliverance of his people. 7 Through his exploits he caused bitterness to several kings and joy to Jacob, and his memory is forever blessed. 8 He went through the cities of Judah and exterminated the wicked, and he turned away Israel's wrath. 9 His name became famous to the ends of the earth, and he gathered those who were about to perish. 10 Apollonius assembled pagan troops, a large army drawn from Samaria, to fight Israel. 11 As soon as Judas learned of this, he marched against him, defeated him, and killed him. A great number of enemies perished, and the rest fled. 12 The Jews seized their loot and Judas took Apollonius' sword and always used it in battles from then on. 13 Seron, commander of the Syrian army, having learned that Judas had gathered a large group of loyal Jews marching with him into battle, 14 He said, "I will make a name for myself and gain glory in the kingdom; I will fight Judas and those with him who despise the king's orders."« 15 So he made a second expedition, and with him assembled a powerful army of impious men, to help him and take revenge on the children of Israel. 16 When they were close to the ascent of Béthoron, Judas marched to meet them with a small troop. 17 When his men saw the army advancing against them, they said to Judas, "How can we, so few in number, fight against such a powerful multitude, especially since we are exhausted from fasting today?"« 18 Judas replied: "It is easy for a multitude to be locked in the hands of a few, for the God of heaven there is no difference in saving by a large number or by a small number. 19 For victory in war does not lie in the multitude of combatants, but strength comes from heaven. 20 They are coming against us, filled with pride and impiety, to destroy us, our wives and our children, and to plunder us. 21 But we are fighting for our lives and for our law. 22 God will crush them before us, so do not be afraid of them.» 23 As soon as he had finished speaking, he suddenly threw himself upon them: Seron was defeated and saw his army crushed before his eyes. 24 Judas pursued him down the descent from Bethoron to the plain, eight hundred men of their troops were killed and the rest fled to the land of the Philistines. 25 Then began to spread the fear of Judas and his brothers, and terror among the surrounding nations. 26 His name reached the king, and all the peoples spoke of Judas's battles. 27 When King Antiochus learned of this news, he was overcome with anger, he gave orders and gathered all the troops of his kingdom, a very powerful army. 28 He opened his treasury and gave his troops a year's pay and commanded them to be ready for anything. 29 Then he realized that money was lacking in his coffers and the taxes from the province brought in little, because of the troubles and evils he had unleashed in the country by wanting to abolish the laws that had been in use since ancient times. 30 He feared that, as had happened several times, he would not have the means to provide for the expenses and liberality that he had previously lavished in abundance and more widely than all the kings who had preceded him. 31 In this extreme predicament, he resolved to go to Persia to collect tribute from those provinces and raise a great deal of money. 32 He therefore left Lysias, a person of considerable importance and of the royal family, in charge of the affairs of the kingdom, from the Euphrates River to the borders of Egypt., 33 and to take care of his son Antiochus until his return. 34 He entrusted him with half of his troops and the elephants and gave him orders for the execution of all his designs and especially concerning all the inhabitants of Judea and Jerusalem. 35 Lysias was to send an army against them to break and annihilate the power of Israel and the remnant of Jerusalem, and to erase their memory from that place., 36 and to establish throughout their country sons of foreigners, to whom he would distribute their lands by lot. 37 Then, having taken with him the other half of his troops, the king left Antioch, his capital, in the year one hundred and forty-seven, crossed the Euphrates river and traversed the high country. 38 Lysias chose Ptolemy, son of Dorymenes, Nicanor and Gorgias, skilled captains and friends of the king, 39 and he sent with them forty thousand foot soldiers and seven thousand horsemen, to invade the land of Judah and destroy it according to the king's order. 40 They set out with all their troops and, having entered Judea, they camped near Emmaus, in the plain. 41 When the merchants of the land heard of their arrival, they took with them much silver and gold, as well as fetters, and went to the Syrian camp to buy the Israelites as slaves. The troops from Syria and the land of the Philistines joined this army. 42 Judas and his brothers, seeing that the situation had worsened and that the enemy armies were encamped at their borders, and having also learned of the king's order to destroy and exterminate their people, 43 They said to one another, "Let us rebuild the ruins of our people and fight for our people and our sanctuary."« 44 The assembly therefore gathers to be ready for battle and to pray and implore pity and mercy. 45 But Jerusalem was without inhabitants, like a desert; none of her children entered it or left it. The sanctuary was trampled underfoot, and foreigners occupied the stronghold; it was a dwelling place for the nations. Jacob's joy was gone; the flute and harp were silent. 46 Having gathered together, they came to Maspha, opposite Jerusalem, because there was formerly in Maspha a place of prayer for Israel. 47 They fasted that day, covered themselves with sackcloth, threw ashes on their heads, and tore their clothes. 48 They spread out the book of the law, which the nations were searching for in order to paint images of their idols on it. 49 They brought the priestly garments, the firstfruits and the tithes, and summoned some Nazarenes who had completed the time of their vow, 50 And they cried out with a loud voice to heaven, saying, «What shall we do for these men, and where shall we lead them?” 51 Your sanctuary has been trampled and desecrated, and your priests are in mourning and humiliation. 52 And now the nations have gathered against us to destroy us. You know their plans against us. 53 How can we stand up to them if you don't support us?» 54 And they sounded the trumpets and gave a loud shout. 55 Then Judas appointed leaders of the people: leaders of a thousand men, of a hundred, of fifty, and of ten. 56And he told those who had just built a house to take wives and plant a vineyard, and those who were afraid to return each to his home, according to the law. 57 Then the army set off and went to camp south of Emmaus. 58 There Judas said to them, «Gird yourselves and be brave and be ready for tomorrow morning to fight against these nations gathered to destroy us and our sanctuary. 59 For it is better for us to die with weapons in hand than to see the suffering of our people and our sanctuary desecrated. 60 Whatever heaven wills, let it be done.»

1 Maccabees 4

1 Gorgias took with him five thousand men and a thousand elite cavalrymen, and they set out during the night., 2 To approach the Jewish camp and strike them unexpectedly, the men from the fortress of Zion served as their guides. 3 Judas, having learned of this, rose up, he and the valiant men, to attack the king's army which was at Emmaus, 4 while the troops were still dispersed outside the camp. 5 Gorgias arrived at Judah's camp during the night, but he found no one there, so he went looking for them in the mountains, saying, "They are fleeing before us."« 6 As soon as day broke, Judas appeared on the plain with three thousand men, but they did not have the weapons they would have wanted, either for cover or for striking. 7 Upon seeing the fortified camp of the nations, the soldiers covered in breastplates, and the horsemen patrolling around them, all trained for combat, 8 Judas said to the men who were with him: «Do not fear their multitude and do not dread their attack. 9 Remember how our fathers were saved in the Red Sea, when Pharaoh was pursuing them with a powerful army. 10 Let us now cry out to heaven, hoping that he will deign to have mercy on us, remember his covenant with our fathers, and destroy this army before our very eyes today. 11 And all nations will know that there is someone who delivers and saves Israel.» 12 Then the strangers looked up and saw them marching against them, 13 And they went out of the camp to give battle, at the same time those who were with Judas sounded the trumpet. 14 They came to blows and the nations were defeated and fled into the plain. 15 The last ranks all fell by the sword and the Jews pursued them as far as Gazara and into the plains of Judea, Azotus and Jamnia and they killed nearly three thousand of them. 16 Then Judas, with his army, retraced his steps and ceased pursuing them., 17 saying to the people: «Do not be greedy for plunder, for a battle awaits us. 18 Gorgias and his troops are near us in the mountains, but hold firm now against our enemies, defeat them, and then you can take their plunder without fear.» 19 Judas was still speaking when a division of Gorgias appeared coming out of the mountain. 20 They saw that their people were fleeing and that the Jews had set fire to the camp, for the smoke that could be seen showed what had happened. 21 At this sight, they were greatly afraid, and as they also saw Judah's army lined up on the plain, ready to give battle, 22 They all fled to the land of the Philistines. 23 Judas returned to plunder the camp; they carried off much gold and silver, as well as purple and scarlet fabrics and great riches. 24 Upon their return, they sang hymns, offering up praises to the Lord: "For he is good, for his mercy endures forever."« 25 A great deliverance was given to Israel on that day. 26 Those foreigners who had escaped came to tell Lysias everything that had happened. 27 Upon learning this news, he was saddened and dejected, because his plans against Israel had failed and the king's orders were not being carried out. 28 The following year, Lysias assembled an army of sixty thousand elite infantry and five thousand cavalry, in order to subdue the Jews. 29 They advanced towards Judea and set up camp near Beth-horon. Judas marched against them at the head of ten thousand men. 30 At the sight of this formidable army, he prayed, saying: «Blessed are you, O deliverer of Israel, who have broken the strength of the giant by the hand of your servant David and delivered the camp of the Philistines into the hands of Jonathan, son of Saul, and his armor-bearer. 31 Enclose this army in the hands of your people Israel, and let them be confused with their infantry and cavalry. 32 Inspire terror in them, crush their presumptuous audacity, and let them be shaken by their defeat. 33 "May they fall by the sword of those who love you, and may all who know your name offer you hymns of praise."» 34 They engaged in battle and five thousand men of Lysias' army fell before the Jews. 35 Seeing the rout of his army and the intrepidity of Judah's soldiers, who showed themselves willing to live or die honorably, Lysias returned to Antioch and recruited foreigners, promising himself, after increasing his army, to return to Judea. 36 Then Judas and his brothers said, "Now that our enemies are defeated, let us go up and purify the temple and reconsecrate it."« 37 The whole army gathered and they went up Mount Zion. 38 Seeing the sanctuary deserted, the altar desecrated, the gates burned, shrubs growing in the courtyard as in a forest or on the mountains, and the chambers destroyed, 39 They tore their clothes, mourned bitterly, and poured ashes on their heads, 40 They prostrated themselves with their faces to the ground and, while the trumpets sounded in a fanfare, shouted towards the sky. 41 Then Judas detached a corps of troops to fight the Syrians who were in the citadel, until the holy places were purified. 42 Then he chose priests without blemish, devoted to the law of God, 43 And they purified the sanctuary and removed the defiled stones to an unclean place. 44 They deliberated on what should be done to the altar of burnt offerings which had been desecrated, 45 And the happy thought came to them to destroy it, lest it become a disgrace to them after the pagans had defiled it. They demolished the altar, 46 and they placed the stones on the temple mountain, in a suitable place, awaiting the coming of a prophet who would give a decision concerning them. 47 And they took uncut stones, according to the law, and built a new altar on the pattern of the old one. 48 They rebuilt the sanctuary, as well as the interior of the temple, and they consecrated the courtyards. 49 They made new sacred utensils, and put the candelabra, the altar of incense, and the table back in the temple. 50 They burned incense on the altar, lit the lamps of the menorah, and they gave light in the temple. 51 They placed loaves of bread on the table and hung the sails. After they had finished all the work they had done, 52 They got up very early on the twenty-fifth day of the ninth month, the month named Casleu, in the year one hundred and forty-eight., 53 and they offered a sacrifice, according to the law, on the new altar of burnt offerings which they had built. 54 In the same month and on the same day that it had been profaned by the nations, the altar was consecrated again, with the singing of psalms, and the sound of harps, lyres and cymbals. 55 All the people fell on his face and worshipped him, and raising their eyes to heaven, they blessed the One who had given him prosperity. 56 They celebrated the dedication of the altar for eight days and offered burnt offerings with joy and sacrifices of thanksgiving and praise. 57 They adorned the temple facade with crowns and shields and repaired the temple entrances and chambers and fitted them with doors. 58 There was great joy among the people, and the reproach inflicted by the nations was removed. 59 Judas, in agreement with his brothers and the whole assembly of Israel, established that the days of the dedication of the altar would be celebrated in their time each year for eight days, starting from the twenty-fifth of Casleu, with joy and gladness. 60 At that time they built on Mount Zion a high wall and strong towers, so that the nations would no longer come and trample the holy places underfoot, as they had done before. 61 And Judah stationed a detachment there for its guard and defense. Beth-sur was fortified so that the people would have a fortress opposite Idumea.

1 Maccabees 5

1 When the surrounding nations learned that the altar had been rebuilt and the sanctuary restored to its former state, they were very angry. 2 They resolved to exterminate the descendants of Jacob who lived among them, and they began to massacre many of them and pursue them. 3 Judas waged war against the sons of Esau in Idumea, in the land of Acrabathane, because they attacked the children of Israel; 1 Maccabees 14

1 In the year one hundred and seventy-two, King Demetrius assembled his armies and went to Media to recruit auxiliary troops to fight Tryphon. 2 Arsaces, king of Persia and Media, having learned that Demetrius had entered his territory, sent one of his generals to capture him alive. 3 He set out and, having defeated Demetrius' army, he seized him and brought him to Arsaces, who put him in prison. 4 The land of Judah was at peace throughout the days of Simon. He strove to bring prosperity to his people, and his authority and glory pleased the people throughout those days. 5Not to mention his other achievements, he took Joppa and made it a port which connected him with the islands of the sea. 6 He expanded the borders of his nation and became master of the country. 7 He gathered a large number of prisoners, he seized Gazara, Bethsur and the citadel, from which he removed all the filth, and there was no one who could resist him. 8 Each person cultivated their land in peace; the soil yielded its produce and the trees in the fields their fruit. 9 The old men, sitting in the public squares, all talked about the prosperity of the country, and the young men donned war clothes as an ornament. 10 Simon distributed supplies to the cities and provided them with everything necessary for defense: to the point that his glorious name was famous to the ends of the earth. 11 He restored peace to his country and Israel rejoiced with great joy. 12 Each one sat under his own vine and fig tree, and no one inspired fear in them. 13 There was no longer any adversary to attack them in the country; the enemy kings were defeated in those days. 14 He was the support of all the unfortunate of his people, he showed zeal for the law and eliminated all the impious and wicked. 15 He glorified the sanctuary and multiplied the sacred utensils. 16 When news of Jonathan's death reached Rome and even Sparta, they were deeply grieved. 17 But when they learned that Simon, his brother, was high priest in his place and ruler of the whole land and all its cities, 18 They wrote to him on bronze tablets to renew with him the alliance and friendship they had made with Judas and Jonathan, his brothers. 19 The letters were read in the presence of the entire assembly in Jerusalem, and here is a copy of the one the Spartans sent: 20 «"The leaders of the Spartans and the city to Simon the high priest, to the elders, the priests, and the rest of the Jewish people, their brothers: Greetings. 21 The ambassadors who were sent to our people told us of the glory and honor you enjoy, and we rejoiced at their arrival. 22 And we have recorded among the decisions of the people what was said by them, namely: Numenius, son of Antiochus and Antipater, son of Jason, ambassadors of the Jews, came to us to renew friendship with us. 23And it pleased the people to receive these men with honor and to deposit a copy of their speeches in the public archives, so that the people of Sparta might preserve the memory of them, and we had this copy written down for Simon the high priest.» 24 After that, Simon sent Numenius to Rome, with a large gold shield weighing a thousand minae, to secure the alliance with them. 25 When the people heard these things, they said, «What thanks shall we give to Simon and his sons? 26 For he showed unwavering steadfastness; he, his brothers, and his father's house fought and drove out the enemies of Israel and secured their freedom.» They engraved these things on bronze tablets, which they hung on a pillar on Mount Zion. 27 Here is the copy: «On the eighteenth day of the month of Elul, in the year one hundred and seventy-two, in the third year of Simon the high priest, in Saramel, 28 In the great assembly of priests and people, of the princes of the nation and the elders of the land, the following was proclaimed: «In the many battles which have taken place in our country, 29 Simon son of Mattathias, of the descendants of Jarib and his brothers, exposed themselves to danger and resisted the enemies of their nation, so that their sanctuary and the law might remain standing, and they obtained great glory for their nation. 30 Jonathan gathered his nation and became their high priest, then he was reunited with his people. 31 Their enemies wanted to trample their country and devastate it and lay hands on their sanctuary. 32 So Simon arose and fought for his nation; he spent much of his own wealth, provided weapons to the valiant men of his nation, and gave them a salary., 33 He fortified the cities of Judea, as well as Bethsuri, located on the border, where the weapons of the enemies had previously been kept, and he placed a garrison of Jewish troops there. 34 He fortified Joppa, located on the sea, and Gazara on the border of Azotus, formerly inhabited by the enemies, and he established Jews there and supplied them with everything necessary for their recovery. 35 The people saw Simon's conduct and the glory he intended to give to his nation, and they made him their leader and high priest because of all the services he had rendered them, and because of the justice and faithfulness he maintained toward his nation, and because he worked in every way to exalt his people. 36 «While he lived, everything prospered in his hands, to the point that he drove out the nations from the land they occupied, as well as those who were in the city of David in Jerusalem, who had built themselves a citadel from which they made sorties, defiling the surroundings of the sanctuary and greatly profaning its holiness. 37 He settled Jewish warriors there and fortified it to ensure the defense of the country and the city, and he raised the walls of Jerusalem. 38 King Demetrius consequently granted him the supreme priesthood, 39 He declared him his friend and bestowed upon him the highest honors. 40 For he had learned that the Romans called the Jews friends and allies and brothers and that they had honorably received Simon's envoys. 41 «"So the Jews and the priests were pleased that Simon should be prince and high priest forever, until a trustworthy prophet should appear, 42 that he command their armies, that he take care of sacred things, that he appoint officers for public services, to administer the country, to oversee armaments and defend fortresses, 43 that he take care of holy things, that he be obeyed by all, that all public acts in the country be written in his name, and that he be clothed in purple and gold. 44 No one from the people or from among the priests shall be permitted to reject any of these points, to contradict any order given by him, to convene any assembly in the land without his permission, to wear a purple robe or a gold clasp. 45 Anyone who acts contrary to this decree or violates any article thereof will incur punishment. 46 «"It seemed good to the people to invest Simon with the power to act according to this decree. 47 Simon accepted; he was willing to fulfill the functions of high priest, commander of the armies, and governor of the Jews and priests, and to exercise supreme command.» 48 It was decided to engrave this document on bronze tablets and place them prominently in the temple gallery., 49 and to place a copy of it in the treasury, for use by Simon and his sons.

1 Maccabees 15

1 King Antiochus, son of Demetrius, sent a letter from the islands of the sea to Simon, high priest and governor of the Jews, and to the entire nation, 2 It was worded as follows: "King Antiochus, to Simon, high priest and governor, and to the nation of the Jews: greetings. 3 Since wretches have seized the kingdom of our fathers, and I wish to reclaim it in order to restore it to its former state, and since I have gathered numerous troops and equipped many warships, 4 intending to land in the country to take revenge on those who have ruined our country and devastated many cities in this kingdom, 5 I confirm to you all the tribute remissions granted to you by my predecessor kings and all the other remissions they have granted you. 6 I allow you to mint coins bearing your imprint for your country. 7 May Jerusalem and the temple be free, and may all the weapons you have made and the fortresses you have built and occupy remain in your possession. 8 May everything owed or due to the royal treasury be given to you now and forever. 9 When we have regained possession of our kingdom, we will magnificently honor you, your nation, and the sanctuary, so that your glory will shine throughout the universe.» 10 In the year one hundred and seventy-four, Antiochus set out for the land of his fathers and all the troops came to rally around him, so that few men remained at Tryphon. 11 King Antiochus went in pursuit of him and Tryphon came fleeing to Dora, on the sea. 12 For he saw that evils were piling up upon him and that his army was abandoning him. 13 Antiochus came to camp in front of Dora with one hundred and twenty thousand combatants and eight thousand horsemen. 14 He besieged the city and, as ships approached from the sea, he pressed upon it both by land and by sea, letting no one enter or leave. 15 However, Numenius and those who had accompanied him arrived from the city of Rome with letters addressed to kings and countries; here is their content: 16 «Lucius, and by the Jewish people. 18 They brought a shield of gold worth a thousand mines. 19 That is why it seemed good to us to write to kings and countries not to cause them harm, not to attack them, their cities, or their country, and not to lend assistance to those who would wage war against them. 20 It seemed good to us to receive the shield from them. 21 »If any wicked men have fled from their own and building machines, and he imprisoned Tryphon so that no one could enter or leave. 26 So Simon sent him a relief force of two thousand elite men, as well as money, gold, and considerable supplies. 27 The king refused to receive them, but he revoked all previous commitments he had made to Simon and treated him as a stranger. 28 He sent Athenobius, one of his friends, to communicate with him and that, we will go to war against you.» 32 Athenobius, a friend of the king, having arrived in Jerusalem, saw the magnificence of Simon, a sideboard covered with gold and silver vases and the great pomp with which he was surrounded, he was astonished and he repeated the words of the king. 33 Simon answered him, «It is not foreign land that we have taken, nor have we seized the possessions of others, but the inheritance of our fathers, which for a time was unjustly possessed by our enemies. 34 For us, finding the opportunity favorable, we claim the heritage of our fathers. 35 As for Joppa and Gazara, which you claim, these two cities did much harm to our people and in our own land; we will give one hundred talents for them.» Athenobius did not answer him a word., 36 But he returned angrily to the king and reported Simon's reply, the magnificence of his court, and all that he had seen, which threw the king into great anger. 37 However, Tryphon fled to Orthosias on a ship. 38 The king war against the people. The king, however, pursued Tryphon. 40 Cendébée, having gone to Jamnia, began to anger the people, to invade Judea, to take prisoners and to massacre. He fortified Gédor 41 and he placed cavalry and foot troops there, to make sorties and infest the roads of Judea, as the king had commanded him.

1 Maccabees 16

1 John came up from Gazara and told his father what Cendébée was doing. 2 Simon called his two eldest sons, Judah and John, and said to them, «My brothers and I and my father’s house have fought against the enemies of Israel from our youth until this day, and we have often succeeded by our own hands in saving Israel. 3 Now I am old, and you, by the grace of God, are old enough; take my place and that of my brother, go and fight for our nation, and may heaven's help be with you.» 4 Then he chose twenty thousand fighters and horsemen from the country, who marched against Cendébée, they camped the night at Modin. 5 Having risen in the morning, they advanced towards the plain and behold, a large army of infantry and cavalry came to meet them, separated by the bed of a torrent. 6 John and his men set up camp opposite them. Noticing that his troops were trembling to cross the torrent, he crossed it first; having seen this, his warriors crossed after him. 7 He divided his army into two corps, placing the cavalry between the infantry, but the enemy cavalry was very numerous. 8 They sounded the trumpets and Cendébée was put to flight with his army, many fell struck down and the rest sought refuge in the fortress. 9 Then Judas, John's brother, was wounded, but John pursued the fugitives until he came to Gedor, which Cendébée had fortified. 10 The vanquished fled to the towers in the fields of Azotus, and he set the city on fire. Two thousand of them perished, and John returned in peace to Judea. 11 Ptolemy, son of Abobus, had been appointed military governor of the plain of Jericho; he possessed a great deal of gold and silver., 12 because he was the son-in-law of the high priest. 13 His heart became proud, he aspired to become master of the land, and he plotted treacherous schemes against Simon and his sons, to destroy them. 14 Now Simon, who was inspecting the cities of Judah, concerned himself with their welfare, went down to Jericho, he, Mattathias his son and Judah, in the year one hundred and seventy-seven, in the eleventh month, which is the month of Sabbath. 15 Abobus' son received them by trickery in a small fortress, named Doch, which he had built, he prepared a great feast for them and kept men hidden there. 16 When Simon and his sons became drunk, Ptolemy got up with his men and, seizing their weapons, they rushed upon Simon in the banquet hall and massacred him along with his two sons and some servants. 17 He thus committed a great betrayal and returned evil for good. 18 Ptolemy immediately wrote to the king to inform him of the event and to ask him to send troops to his aid, so that he would hand over the country and the cities of the Jews. 19 He dispatched other emissaries to Gazara to kill John and sent letters to the generals, summoning them to him, to give them money, gold and gifts. 20 He sent still others to occupy Jerusalem and the temple mountain. 21 But a messenger, having gone ahead, came to announce to John, in Gazara, the murder of his father and brothers, and he added: "He has also sent assassins to kill you.". 22 At this news, John was greatly disturbed; he seized the men who came to kill him and had them killed, for he recognized that they intended to kill him. 23 The rest of John's story, his wars, the exploits he accomplished, the walls he had built, and all his actions, 24 This is all written in the Annals of his high priesthood, from the day he became high priest after his father.
