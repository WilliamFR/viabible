Proverbs 1

1 Proverbs of Solomon, son of David, king of Israel: 2 to know wisdom and instruction, to understand sensible speech, 3 to acquire enlightened education, justice, fairness, and integrity, 4 to give discernment to the simple, and knowledge and reflection to the young. 5 Let the wise listen and gain knowledge; let the intelligent person know prudent advice., 6 It will include proverbs and mysterious meanings, the maxims of the wise and their riddles. 7 The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom; fools despise wisdom and instruction. 8 Listen, my son, to your father's instruction and do not forsake your mother's teaching., 9 for it is a crown of grace for your head and an ornament for your neck. 10 My son, if sinners want to seduce you, do not give in. 11 If they say, "Come with us, let us set ambushes to shed blood, let us lay traps for the innocent without cause. 12 Let us swallow them up alive, as Sheol does, whole, like those who go down into the pit. 13 We will find all kinds of precious goods, we will fill our houses with plunder, 14 You will draw your share with us; there will only be one purse for all of us.» 15 My son, do not go with them on the road; turn your foot from their path., 16 for their feet run to evil, they hasten to shed blood. 17 It is futile to cast the net before the eyes of all that has wings, 18 They lay traps against their own blood, they set snares for their own souls. 19 Such are the ways of every man greedy for gain; gain brings ruin to those who possess it. 20 Wisdom cries out in the streets, she raises her voice in the squares. 21 She preaches in noisy crossroads, at the entrances to city gates, she speaks her words: 22 «"How long, simple ones, will you love simplicity? How long will mockers delight in mockery and fools hate science?" 23 Turn around to hear my rebuke; behold, I will pour out my spirit upon you, I will make my words known to you. 24 «Because I call out and you resist, because I stretch out my hand and no one pays attention, 25 Since you disregard all my advice and don't want my reprimand, 26 I too will laugh at your misfortune, I will mock you when terror comes upon you., 27 when terror comes upon you like a storm, when misfortune falls upon you like a whirlwind, when distress and anguish come upon you. 28 «"Then they will call me and I will not answer, they will look for me and they will not find me.". 29 Because they hated science and did not desire the fear of the Lord, 30 because they refused my advice and disregarded all my reprimands, 31 They will eat the fruit of their own way and be filled with their own schemes. 32 For the error of the simple kills them, and the security of fools destroys them. 33 But whoever listens to me will rest securely, he will live peacefully, without fear of harm.»

    Proverbs 2

    1 My son, if you receive my words and treasure my commandments within you, 2 making your ear attentive to wisdom and inclining your heart towards understanding, 3 Yes, if you call for prudence and raise your voice to intelligence, 4 If you search for it like money and dig into it as if to discover treasure, 5 Then you will understand the fear of the Lord and you will find the knowledge of God. 6 For the Lord gives wisdom; from his mouth come knowledge and understanding., 7 He preserves happiness for the upright; he is a shield for those who walk in perfection., 8 He protects the paths of justice, he watches over the way of his faithful. 9 Then you will understand justice, fairness, righteousness, and all the paths of goodness. 10 When wisdom enters your heart and knowledge delights your soul, 11 Reflection will watch over you and intelligence will protect you., 12 to deliver you from the way of evil, from the man who speaks perverse words, 13 of those who abandon the straight paths, in order to walk in dark ways, 14 who rejoice in doing evil and find pleasure in the worst perversions, 15 whose paths are winding and follow oblique routes, 16 to deliver you from the woman who belongs to another, from the stranger who uses sweet words, 17 who abandons the companion of his youth and forgets the covenant of his God, 18 because she leans, along with her house, towards death, and her path leads to hell., 19 Of all those who go to her, none return, none find their way back to the paths of life. 20 Thus you will walk in the way of good men and you will keep to the paths of the righteous. 21 For the upright will inhabit the earth, and the blameless will dwell there., 22 But the wicked will be cut off from the earth, and the faithless will be uprooted from it.

      Proverbs 3

      1 My son, do not forget my teachings and let your heart keep my precepts. 2 They will bring you long days, years of life, and peace. 3 May mercy and truth not abandon you, fasten them around your neck, engrave them on the tablet of your heart. 4 In this way you will find favor and have true wisdom in the sight of God and men. 5 Trust in the Lord with all your heart and do not rely on your own understanding. 6 Remember him in all your ways and he will make your paths straight. 7 Do not be wise in your own eyes; fear the Lord and turn away from evil. 8 It will be good for your body and refreshing for your bones. 9 Honor God with your possessions, with the first fruits of all your income. 10 Then your barns will be filled to overflowing and your vats will overflow with new wine. 11 My son, do not despise the Lord's discipline, nor abhor his punishments. 12 For the Lord disciplines the one he loves, as a father disciplines the child he cherishes. 13 Blessed is the man who finds wisdom and the man who acquires understanding. 14 Acquiring it is better than acquiring silver, possessing it better than possessing pure gold. 15 It is more precious than pearls; all jewels cannot equal it. 16 In his right hand is long life, in his left, riches and glory. 17 His ways are pleasant ways, all his paths, paths of peace. 18 It is a tree of life for those who grasp it, and he who clings to it is happy. 19 It is by wisdom that the Lord founded the earth, by understanding that he established the heavens. 20 It is through his science that the abysses have opened and the clouds distill the dew. 21 My son, do not let them depart from your sight; keep wisdom and reflection., 22 They will be the life of your soul and the ornament of your neck. 23 Then you will walk safely along your path and your foot will not stumble. 24 If you lie down, you will be without fear, and when you lie down, your sleep will be sweet. 25 You will have nothing to fear from sudden terror or an attack from the wicked. 26 For the Lord will be your assurance and he will keep your foot from every snare. 27 Do not refuse a benefit to those to whom it is due, when it is in your power to grant it. 28 Don't say to your neighbor, "Go and come back, tomorrow I'll give," when you can give right now. 29 Do not plot evil against your neighbor when he remains peaceful near you. 30 Don't argue with someone without reason, especially if they haven't done you any harm. 31 Do not envy the man violence and do not choose any of his paths: 32 for the Lord detests the perverse, but with the upright in heart is his intimacy. 33 The Lord's curse is on the house of the wicked, but he blesses the home of the righteous. 34 He mocks the mockers and gives grace to the humble. 35 Glory will be the portion of the wise, but shame will be the portion of the foolish.

      Proverbs 4

      1 Listen, my sons, to a father's instruction and pay attention, so that you may learn wisdom, 2 For I give you good teaching: do not abandon my teaching. 3 I too was a son to my father, a tender and only son to my mother. 4 He instructed me and said to me, «Let your heart retain my words, observe my precepts and you will live. 5 Acquire wisdom, acquire understanding, do not forget the words of my mouth and do not turn away from them. 6 Don't abandon her and she will keep you, love her and she will keep you. 7 This is the beginning of wisdom: Get wisdom, and out of all that you have, get understanding. 8 Hold her in high esteem and she will exalt you, she will bring you glory, if you embrace her. 9 She will place a crown of grace upon your head, she will adorn you with a magnificent diadem.» 10 Listen, my son, and receive my words, and the years of your life will multiply. 11 I will teach you the way of wisdom, I will lead you in the paths of righteousness. 12 If you walk, your steps will not be cramped, and if you run, you will not stumble. 13 Hold on to the instruction, don't abandon it, keep it because it is your life. 14 Do not enter the path of the wicked, nor walk in the way of evil men. 15 Avoid it, don't go through it, turn away from it and go on. 16 For they do not sleep unless they do evil; their sleep flees unless they cause someone to stumble. 17 For they eat the bread of crime, they drink the wine of violence. 18 The path of the righteous is like the bright morning light, which grows brighter and brighter until the day breaks. 19 The way of the wicked is like darkness; they do not see what will cause them to stumble. 20 My son, pay attention to my words, listen carefully to my speeches. 21 Do not let them depart from your sight, keep them in the midst of your heart. 22 For they are life to those who find them, health to their whole body. 23 Guard your heart above all else, for from it spring the fountains of life. 24 Keep deceitful speech out of your mouth and falsehood far from your lips. 25 Let your eyes look straight ahead and let your eyelids turn in front of you. 26 Make a level path for your feet, and let all your ways be straight. 27 Do not incline to the right or to the left, and turn your foot away from evil.

        Proverbs 5

        1 My son, pay attention to my wisdom and listen to my understanding, 2 so that you may retain understanding and your lips may preserve knowledge. 3 For the lips of the stranger distill honey and her palate is sweeter than oil. 4 But in the end it is bitter like absinthe, sharp like a double-edged sword. 5 His feet descend towards death, his steps go straight to Sheol. 6 She does not consider the path of life, her steps wander uncertainly, she does not know where. 7 And now, my sons, listen to me and do not turn away from the words of my mouth. 8 Keep your distance from her, do not approach the door of her house, 9 lest you surrender the flower of your youth and your years to others at the hands of a cruel tyrant, 10 lest strangers should become filled with your possessions and the fruit of your labor should pass into the house of another, 11 lest you should groan in the end, when your flesh and body are consumed, 12 and that you do not say: How then could I have hated discipline, and how could my heart have scorned reproof? 13 How could I have failed to listen to the voice of my teachers, failed to lend an ear to those who were instructing me? 14 I almost reached the height of misfortune, in the midst of the people and the assembly. 15 Drink the water from your cistern, the streams that flow from your well. 16 Let your springs overflow outside, let your streams flow in the public squares. 17 Let them be for you alone and not for strangers with you. 18 May your spring be blessed and find joy in the wife of your youth. 19 Charming doe, graceful gazelle, may her charms intoxicate you at all times, be always enamored of her love. 20 Why, my son, would you fall in love with a stranger and kiss the breast of an unknown woman? 21 For before the eyes of the Lord are the ways of man; he considers all his paths. 22 The wicked man is caught in his own iniquities; he is seized by the bonds of his sin. 23 He will die for lack of correction; he will be deceived by the excess of his madness.

          Proverbs 6

          1 My son, if you have stood surety for your friend, if you have given your word to a stranger, 2 If you are bound by the words of your mouth, if you are caught by the words of your mouth, 3 Do this, my friend: free yourself. Since you have fallen into the hands of your neighbor, go, prostrate yourself and press him hard. 4 Give neither sleep to your eyes, nor slumber to your eyelids, 5 Break free, like a gazelle from the hand of the hunter, like a bird from the hand of the fowler. 6 Go to the ant, O sluggard, consider its ways and become wise. 7 She who has neither chief, nor works inspector, nor sovereign, 8 It gathers its food in the summer, it collects its sustenance at harvest time. 9 How long, O sluggard, will you lie down? When will you get up from your sleep? 10 «"A little sleep, a little dozing, a little resting your hands on your bed."» 11 And your poverty will come like a traveler, and your want like an armed man. 12 A perverse man, an unjust man, walks with perversity in his mouth., 13 He blinks his eyes, scratches his foot, and makes signs with his fingers. 14 Perversity is in his heart; he plots evil at all times; he stirs up quarrels. 15 Therefore, his downfall will come suddenly; he will be broken all at once and without remedy. 16 There are six things the Lord hates, seven things he abhors: 17 haughty eyes, a lying tongue, hands that spill innocent blood, 18 a heart that plots sinful schemes, feet eager to rush into evil, 19 the false witness who utters lies and the one who sows discord among brothers. 20 My son, keep your father's command and do not reject your mother's teaching. 21 Bind them constantly to your heart, fasten them around your neck. 22 He will guide you in your walk, he will watch over you in your sleep, and when you wake up, he will converse with you. 23 For the precept is a lamp and the law a light, and the warnings that instruct are the way of life. 24 They will protect you from the wicked woman, from the sweet tongue of the stranger. 25 Do not covet her beauty in your heart, and let her not seduce you with her eyelids. 26 Because for the courtesan one is reduced to a piece of bread, and the married woman traps a precious life. 27 Is it possible for a man to put fire in his bosom without his clothes catching fire? 28 Or shall a man walk on hot coals and not have his feet burned? 29 So it is with the one who goes to his neighbor's wife: whoever touches her will not go unpunished. 30 We do not despise a thief who steals to satisfy his hunger when he has nothing to eat: 31 Surprised, he gives back seven times as much, he gives everything he has in his house. 32 But he who corrupts a woman is senseless; he destroys himself; he who acts in this way, 33 He reaps only wounds and ignominy, and his disgrace will not be erased. 34 For jealousy stirs the fury of the wronged man; he is merciless on the day of vengeance., 35 He doesn't care about any ransom, he doesn't want one, even if you were to multiply the gifts.

            Proverbs 7

            1 My son, remember my words and keep my precepts with you. 2 Observe my precepts and you will live; keep my teaching as the apple of your eye. 3 Link them on your fingers, write them on the table of your heart. 4 Say to wisdom: You are my sister, and call understanding your friend., 5 so that she may protect you from another man's wife, from the stranger who whispers sweet words. 6 Standing at the window of my house, I was looking through the latticework. 7 Among the fools I saw, among the young men I noticed, a boy devoid of sense. 8 He was walking along the street, near the lodgings of a stranger, and he was approaching her house. 9 It was at dusk, at the fall of day, in the middle of the night and darkness. 10 And then a woman approaches him, dressed like a prostitute and with deceit in her heart. 11 She is impetuous and untamable; her feet cannot rest in her home., 12 Sometimes in the street, sometimes in the squares and near all corners, she keeps watch. 13 She grabbed the young man and kissed him, and with a cheeky face said to him: 14 «"I was supposed to offer peaceful victims, today I have fulfilled my vows.". 15 That's why I went out to meet you, to look for you, and I found you. 16 I lined my bed with blankets and Egyptian cotton rugs. 17 I perfumed my bed with myrrh, aloe, and cinnamon. 18 Come, let us be intoxicated with love until morning, let us surrender to the delights of pleasure. 19 Because my husband is not at home, he has gone on a long trip., 20 He took the bag of money with him; he won't return home until the full moon.» 21 She seduces him with her words, she entices him with the words on her lips., 22 He immediately begins to follow her, like an ox going to the slaughter, like a fool running towards the punishment of fetters, 23 until an arrow pierces his liver, like a bird rushing into a net, unaware that its life is at stake. 24 And now, my sons, listen to me and pay attention to the words of my mouth. 25 Do not let your heart turn to his ways, do not stray into his paths. 26 Because it caused many injuries and many victims. 27 His house is the road to Sheol, which leads down to the abode of death.

              Proverbs 8

              1 Does not wisdom cry out, does not understanding raise her voice? 2 It is at the top of the heights, on the road, at the junction of paths, that she positions herself, 3 Near the gates, on the outskirts of the city, at the entrance to the gates, she makes her voice heard: 4 «"Men, it is to you that I cry out, and my voice is addressed to the children of men.". 5 Simple people, learn prudence; fools, learn intelligence. 6 Listen, for I have wonderful things to say, and my lips are open to teach what is right. 7 «For my mouth speaks the truth, and my lips abhor iniquity.”. 8 All the words of my mouth are just; there is nothing false or crooked in them. 9 All are just for the intelligent and upright for those who have found knowledge. 10 Receive my instruction rather than money, and knowledge rather than pure gold. 11 For wisdom is worth more than pearls, and the most precious objects cannot equal it. 12 «I, wisdom, dwell with prudence and possess the knowledge of reflection. 13 The fear of the Lord is hatred of evil, arrogance and pride, the way of evil and the perverse mouth, these are what I hate. 14 The advice and the success belong to me, I am the intelligence, the strength is mine. 15 Through me kings reign and princes decree what is just. 16 Through me rule the chiefs and the great men, all the judges of the earth. 17 I love those who love me, and those who earnestly seek me find me. 18 With me are riches and honor, lasting wealth and righteousness. 19 My fruit is better than gold, than pure gold, and what comes from me is better than refined silver. 20 I walk in the path of righteousness, in the midst of the paths of judgment, 21 to give possessions to those who love me and to fill their treasures. 22 «The Lord possessed me at the beginning of his ways, before his most ancient works. 23 I was founded from eternity, from the beginning, before the origins of the earth. 24 There were no deeps when I was born, no springs full of water. 25 Before the mountains were settled, before the hills, I was brought forth, 26 when he had not yet made the earth, nor the plains, nor the first elements of the dust of the globe. 27 When he established the heavens, I was there; when he drew a circle on the face of the deep, 28 when he established the clouds above and subdued the springs of the deep, 29 when he fixed its limit to the sea, so that the waters would not transgress its boundaries, when he laid the foundations of the earth. 30 I worked alongside him, rejoicing every day and constantly playing in his presence, 31 playing on the globe of his earth and finding my delights among the children of men. 32  «And now, my sons, listen to me: blessed are those who keep my ways.”. 33 Listen to instruction to become wise, do not reject it. 34 Blessed is the man who listens to me, who watches over my gates every day and guards their doorposts. 35 for whoever finds me finds life and obtains favor from the Lord. 36 But whoever offends me wounds his own soul, and all who hate me love death.»

                Proverbs 9

She said to the one who lacked sense: 17 «"Stolen waters are sweeter, and the bread of mystery is more pleasing."» 18 And he doesn't know that there are shadows there and that his guests are already in the depths of Sheol.

                                          1 Here are more Proverbs of Solomon, collected by the people of Hezekiah, king of Judah. 2 The glory of God is to conceal things, the glory of kings is to examine them. 3 Heaven in its height, earth in its depth, and the hearts of kings are unfathomable. 4 Remove the dross from the silver and a vessel will come out for the refiner. 5 Remove the wicked from before the king, and his throne will be established in righteousness. 6 Don't put on airs in front of the king and don't try to act like you're in the place of the powerful., 7 for it is better that they tell you, "Come up here," than that they humiliate you before the prince whom your eyes have seen. 8 Don't rush into protesting, lest in the end you not know what to do. 9 When your neighbor has wronged you, defend yourself against your neighbor, but do not reveal another's secret., 10 lest whoever hears of it should shame you and your disgrace should not be erased. 11 Like golden apples in silver settings, so is a word spoken at the right time. 12 Like a gold ring and an ornament of fine gold, so is the wise man who takes back a docile ear. 13 Like the freshness of snow at harvest time, so is the faithful messenger to those who send him; he gladdens the soul of his master. 14 Clouds and wind without rain, such is the man who boasts of deceitful gifts. 15 With patience the judge can be persuaded, and a gentle tongue can break bones. 16 If you find honey, eat only what you need, lest you vomit it up when you are full. 17 Rarely set foot in your neighbor's house, lest, tired of you, he may hate you. 18 A club, a sword and a sharp arrow, such is the man who bears false witness against his neighbor. 19 A broken tooth and a slipping foot, that's the confidence a treacherous person inspires on a day of misfortune. 20 Taking off one's coat on a cold day, pouring vinegar on a wound, so does one who sings songs to an afflicted heart. 21 If your enemy is hungry, give him bread to eat; if he is thirsty, give him water to drink., 22 because you are heaping coals on his head, and the Lord will reward you. 23 The north wind brings forth rain and a tongue that meditates in secret, an irritated face. 24 It is better to live on a rooftop than to stay with a quarrelsome wife. 25 Fresh water for a thirsty person, that is good news from a distant land. 26 A troubled fountain and a corrupt spring, such is the righteous man who stumbles before the wicked. 27 It is not good to eat too much honey, so that whoever wants to fathom divine majesty will be overwhelmed by its glory. 28 A city forced open without walls, such is the man who cannot contain himself.

                                            Proverbs 26

                                            1 Like snow in summer and rain during harvest, so glory is not fitting for a fool. 2 Like the sparrow that escapes, like the swallow that flies away, so the curse without cause does not reach. 3 The whip is for the horse, the bit for the donkey, and the stick for the backs of fools. 4 Do not answer a fool according to his folly, lest you yourself become like him. 5 Answer a fool according to his folly, lest he consider himself wise. 6 He cuts off his feet, he drinks iniquity, he who gives messages to a fool. 7 "Take off the legs of the lame man," is the saying from the mouth of the fool. 8 It is like attaching a stone to a slingshot to give glory to a fool. 9 Like a thorn that pierces the hand of a drunkard, so is a saying in the mouth of fools. 10 Like an archer who wounds everyone, so is he who hires fools and passersby. 11 Like a dog that returns to its vomit, so is a fool who returns to his folly. 12 If you see a man who is wise in his own eyes, you should have more hope for a fool than for him. 13 The lazy man said, "There's a lion on the road, there's a lion in the streets."« 14 The door turns on its hinges, so does the lazy man on his bed. 15 The lazy man puts his hand in the dish and has difficulty bringing it to his mouth. 16 In his eyes, the lazy man is wiser than seven prudent advisors. 17 Like someone who grabs a dog by the ears, so is the passerby who gets worked up in another's quarrel. 18 Like a madman hurling flaming darts, arrows, and death, 19 Such is a man who has deceived his neighbor and says, "Was I not joking?"« 20 For lack of wood, the fire goes out; remove the informer and the quarrel subsides. 21 Coal makes a blaze and wood makes fire: thus a quarrelsome man irritates a discussion. 22 The words of the rapporteur are like sweets, they go down to the very depths of the soul. 23 Silver dross applied to an earthen vessel is like burning lips with an evil heart. 24 He who hates disguises himself with his lips, but he harbors treachery within himself. 25 When he softens his voice, do not trust him, for he has seven abominations in his heart. 26 He may well hide his hatred under dissimulation, but his wickedness will be revealed in the assembly. 27 He who digs a pit falls into it, and the stone rolls back on him who rolls it. 28 A false tongue hates those it hurts, and a flattering mouth causes ruin.

                                              Proverbs 27

                                              1 Do not boast about tomorrow, for you do not know what the following day will bring. 2 Let another praise you and not your own mouth, a stranger and not your own lips. 3 The stone is heavy and the sand is weighty: more than either of them weighs the anger of the fool. 4 Fury is cruel and anger impetuous, but who can stand against jealousy? 5 An open reprimand is better than a hidden friendship. 6 A friend's wounds are inspired by loyalty, but an enemy's kisses are deceitful. 7 He who is full tramples the honeycomb underfoot, but to him who is hungry everything bitter seems sweet. 8 Like the bird that wanders far from its nest, so is the man who wanders far from his home. 9 Oil and perfumes gladden the heart, like the sweetness of a friend whose advice comes from the heart. 10 Do not forsake your friend and your father’s friend, and do not enter your brother’s house in the day of your distress; better is a neighbor who is near than a brother who is far away. 11 My son, become wise and gladden my heart, so that I may answer him who insults me. 12 The prudent man sees evil and hides, the simple pass by and bear the punishment. 13 If someone stands surety for a stranger's debt, demand his garment; if he stands surety for a foreigner, demand pledges. 14 Blessing one's neighbor aloud and early in the morning is considered a curse. 15 A continuous gutter on a rainy day and a quarrelsome woman are alike. 16 He who holds it back holds back the wind, and his hand grasps oil. 17 Iron sharpens iron, and so one man sharpens another. 18 He who looks after his fig tree will eat its fruit, and he who looks after his master will be honored. 19 Just as in water face responds to face, so the heart of man responds to man. 20 Sheol and the abyss are never satisfied, nor are the eyes of man ever satisfied. 21 The crucible is for silver and the furnace for gold; let man likewise test the praise which he receives. 22 Even if you were to pound the fool in a mortar, as one grinds grain with a pestle, his madness would not separate from him. 23 Know the condition of your sheep well, pay close attention to your flock, 24 because wealth does not last forever, nor does a crown last from age to age. 25 But when the grass appeared, when the greenery showed itself, when the mountain hay was gathered, 26 You have lambs to clothe you, goats to pay for a field, 27 You have plenty of goat's milk, for your food and for your household, and for the care of your servants.

                                                Proverbs 28

                                                1 The wicked flee when no one pursues them, but the righteous are as bold as a lion. 2 In a country in revolt, leaders multiply, but with an intelligent and wise man, order endures. 3 A poor man who oppresses the unfortunate is like a violent rain that causes famine. 4 Those who abandon the law praise the wicked, while those who observe it are angry with them. 5 Wicked men do not understand what is right, but those who seek the Lord understand everything. 6 Better is a poor man in his integrity than a man of crooked ways who is rich. 7 He who observes the law is a wise son, but he who feeds debauchers brings shame upon his father. 8 He who increases his wealth by interest and usury amasses it for him who has compassion on the poor. 9 If anyone turns a deaf ear to the law, even their prayer is an abomination. 10 He who leads the upright astray into evil will himself fall into the pit he has dug, but the blameless will possess happiness. 11 The rich man is wise in his eyes, but the intelligent poor man knows it. 12 When the righteous triumph, there is a great celebration; when the wicked rise up, everyone hides. 13 He who conceals his faults will not prosper, but he who confesses them and abandons them will obtain mercy. 14 Blessed is the man who always fears the Lord, but he who hardens his heart will fall into misfortune. 15 A roaring lion and a hungry bear, such is the wicked one who rules over a poor people. 16 The prince without intelligence multiplies oppression, but he who hates greed will have long days. 17 A man covered in another's blood flees to the grave: do not stop him. 18 He who walks in integrity will find salvation, but he who follows crooked paths will fall, never to rise again. 19 He who cultivates his field will be filled with bread, but he who pursues vain things will be filled with poverty. 20 A faithful man will be filled with blessings, but one who is eager to get rich will not escape sin. 21 It is not good to show favoritism between people; for a piece of bread, a man becomes a criminal. 22 The envious man is eager to get rich, he does not know that famine will come upon him. 23 He who corrects someone finds more favor than he who flatters with his tongue. 24 He who steals from his father and mother and says, "It is not a sin," is a companion of a robber. 25 A greedy man stirs up quarrels, but he who trusts in the Lord will be satisfied. 26 He who trusts in his own heart is a fool, but he who walks in wisdom will be saved. 27 He who gives to the poor will not go hungry, but he who closes his eyes will be burdened with curses. 28 When the wicked rise up, everyone hides; when they perish, the righteous multiply.

                                                  Proverbs 29

                                                  1 The blameworthy man who stiffens his neck will be broken suddenly and without remedy. 2 When the righteous multiply, the people rejoice; when the wicked exercise power, the people groan. 3 A man who loves wisdom makes his father glad, but a man who consorts with prostitutes squanders his wealth. 4 A king establishes the country through justice, but one who is greedy for gifts ruins it. 5 The man who flatters his neighbor spreads a net beneath his feet. 6 In the sin of the wicked man there is a snare, but the righteous man is in jubilation and joy. 7 The righteous man knows the cause of the poor, but the wicked man does not understand science. 8 The mockers fan the flames in the city, but the wise calm the anger. 9 If a wise man argues with a fool, whether he gets angry or laughs, there will be no peace. 10 Bloodthirsty men hate the upright man, but righteous men protect his life. 11 The fool gives free rein to his passion, but the wise man calms and restrains it. 12 When the prince listens to lying words, all his servants are wicked. 13 The poor man and the oppressor meet, and it is the Lord who enlightens the eyes of both. 14 A king who faithfully judges the poor will have his throne secured forever. 15 The stick and correction give wisdom, but a child left to his whim brings shame to his mother. 16 When the wicked multiply, crime multiplies, but the righteous will witness their downfall. 17 Discipline your son and he will give you rest and bring delight to your soul. 18 When there is no vision, the people are unrestrained; happy is he who observes the law. 19 You cannot correct a slave with words; even if he understands, he does not obey. 20 If you see a man quick to speak, there is more to be hoped for from a fool than from him. 21 If someone treats their slave softly from childhood, the slave will eventually come to believe he is a son. 22 An angry man stirs up quarrels, and a violent man falls into many sins. 23 A man's pride leads him to humiliation, but the humble in spirit obtains glory. 24 He who shares with a thief hates his soul; he hears the curse and says nothing. 25 The fear of men carries with it a snare, but he who trusts in the Lord is safe. 26 Many people seek the favor of the prince, but justice comes to each one from the Lord. 27 The wicked man is an abomination to the righteous, and he whose way is upright is an abomination to the wicked.

                                                    Proverbs 30

                                                    1 The words of Agur, son of Jakeh, the saying. This man said: I have grown weary to know God, to know God, and I am at the end of my strength. 2 Because I am more stupid than anyone and I don't have the intelligence of a man. 3 I have not learned wisdom and I do not know the science of the Holy One. 4 Who ascends to heaven and who descends? Who gathers the wind in his hands? Who binds the waters in his garment? Who establishes all the ends of the earth? What is his name, and what is the name of his son? Surely you know? 5 Every word of God is tested by fire; he is a shield to those who take refuge in him. 6 Do not add anything to his words, lest he rebuke you and you be found a liar. 7 I ask you two things, do not refuse me before I die: 8 Keep falsehood and lying far from me; give me neither poverty nor riches, but grant me only the bread that is necessary for me. 9 lest, when I am full, I deny you and say, «Who is the Lord?» and become poor and steal and dishonor the name of my God. 10 Do not slander a servant to his master, lest he curse you and you bear the penalty. 11 There is a race that curses its father and does not bless its mother. 12 There is a race that is pure in its own eyes and yet is not cleansed of its defilement. 13 He is a race, how haughty are his gaze and how high his eyelids. 14 There is a race whose teeth are swords and molars knives, to devour the unfortunate from the earth and the needy among men. 15 The leech has two daughters: give, give. Three things are insatiable, four never say: Enough. 16 Sheol, the barren womb, the earth that is not satisfied with water, and the fire that never says: Enough. 17 The eye that mocks a father and scorns obedience to a mother, the ravens of the stream will pierce it and the young eagles will devour it. 18 There are three things that are beyond me, and even four that I don't understand: 19 the eagle's footprint in the heavens, the serpent's footprint on the rock, the ship's footprint in the middle of the sea, and the man's footprint in the young girl. 20 This is the way of an adulterous woman: she eats, and wiping her mouth, she says, «I have done nothing wrong.» 21 Under three things the earth trembles, and under four that it cannot bear: 22 under a slave when he comes to reign and a fool when he is full of bread, 23 under a scorned woman when she marries and under a servant when she inherits from her mistress. 24 There are four very small animals on earth, yet they are very wise: 25 The ants, a people without strength, prepare their food in the summer, 26 The hyraxes, a people without power, make their homes in the rocks, 27 Grasshoppers have no king and they all come out in swarms., 28 You can pick up the lizard with your hand and it is located in the palace of the kings. 29 Three of them have a handsome appearance and four have a graceful gait: 30 The lion, the bravest of animals, never backing down from any adversary, 31 the animal with agile loins, or the goat and the king, whom no one can resist. 32 If you are foolish enough to let pride carry you away, and if you even have the thought of it, put your hand over your mouth, 33 because the pressure of milk produces butter, the pressure of the nose produces blood, and the pressure of anger produces quarrels.
