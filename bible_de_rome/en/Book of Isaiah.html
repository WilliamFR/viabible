Isaiah 1

1 The vision of Isaiah, son of Amos, which he saw concerning Judah and Jerusalem in the days of Uzziah, Jotham, Ahaz and Hezekiah, kings of Judah. 2 Heavens, listen and you, earth, give ear, for the Lord speaks: I have nourished children and brought them up, and they have rebelled against me. 3 The ox knows its owner and the donkey its master's crib, but Israel does not know, my people do not understand. 4 Ah, sinful nation, people laden with iniquity, brood of wicked men, criminal sons. They have forsaken the Lord, they have reviled the Holy One of Israel, they have turned back. 5 Where else can you be struck, if you continue your revolts? The whole head is sick and the whole heart is languishing. 6 From the soles of his feet to the crown of his head there is nothing healthy in him: only wounds, bruises, open sores, which have not been dressed, nor bandaged, nor soothed with oil. 7 Your country is a desert, your cities are consumed by fire, your soil is being devoured by foreigners before your very eyes, the devastation is like the ravages of foreigners. 8 And the daughter of Zion remained like a hut in a vineyard, like a shelter in a cucumber field, like a watchtower. 9 If the Lord of hosts had not left us a small remnant, we would be like Sodom, we would resemble Gomorrah. 10 Listen to the word of the Lord, you judges of Sodom; give ear to the law of our God, you people of Gomorrah. 11 What do I care for the multitude of your sacrifices? says the Lord. I am full of burnt offerings of rams and the fat of well-fed animals; I do not delight in the blood of bulls, lambs, and goats. 12 When you come to present yourself before me, who asked you to tread on my courts? 13 Do not continue to bring me vain offerings; incense is an abomination to me; as for new moons, Sabbaths, and convocations, I cannot see crime and solemn assembly together. 14 My soul hates your new moons and your festivals, they are a burden to me, I am weary of enduring them. 15 When you spread out your hands, I will veil my eyes from you; when you multiply your prayers, I will not listen: Your hands are full of blood. 16 Wash yourselves, purify yourselves, remove the malice of your actions from before my eyes, cease doing evil, 17 Learn to do good, seek justice, correct the oppressor, bring justice to the orphan, plead the widow's cause. 18 Come and let us discuss together: Though your sins are like scarlet, they shall become as white as snow; though they are red like purple, they shall become like wool. 19 If you obey willingly, you will eat the bounty of your country. 20 But if you resist and rebel, you will be eaten by the sword, for the mouth of the Lord has spoken. 21 How did she become a prostitute, the faithful city, full of fairness, in which justice dwelt, and now murderers?. 22 Your silver has turned to dross, your wine has been diluted with water. 23 Your princes are rebels and companions of thieves; they all love gifts and chase after rewards; they do not defend the orphan, nor does the widow's cause come before them. 24 Therefore, declares the Lord, the Lord of hosts, the Mighty One of Israel: Ah, I will take satisfaction from my adversaries and I will avenge myself on my enemies. 25 I will stretch out my hand over you and melt away your dross as with potash, and I will remove all your particles of lead. 26 I will give you judges like those of old and counselors like in the beginning. After that you will be called the city of righteousness, the faithful city. 27 Zion will be redeemed by righteousness, and its converts by justice. 28 But the rebels and sinners will be broken together, and those who forsake the Lord will perish. 29 For they will be ashamed of the sacred trees you have loved, and you will blush for the gardens in which you delight. 30 For you will be like a sacred tree with withered leaves and like a garden without water. 31 And the strong man will be the tinder and his works, the spark; they will both burn together and no one will extinguish it.

Isaiah 2

1 The word that Isaiah, son of Amos, saw concerning Judah and Jerusalem. 2 In the latter days the mountain of the Lord’s house will be established as the highest of the mountains and exalted above the hills, and all nations will stream to it., 3 And many nations will come and say, «Come, let us go up to the mountain of the Lord, to the house of the God of Jacob, he will teach us his ways, and we will walk in his paths.» For out of Zion shall go forth the law, and the word of the Lord from Jerusalem. 4 He will be the arbiter of the peoples and the judge of many nations. They will beat their swords into plowshares and their spears into sickles. Nation will not take up sword against nation, nor will they train for war anymore. 5 House of Jacob, come and let us walk in the light of the Lord. 6 For you have forsaken your people, the house of Jacob, because they have long ago reached their limit, they practice magic like the Philistines, and they give hands to the sons of foreigners. 7 Their country is filled with silver and gold and their treasures are endless, their country is filled with horses and their chariots are countless. 8 Their country is filled with idols; they bow down before the work of their hands, before what their fingers have made. 9 That is why mortals will be humbled and humankind brought low: no, you will not forgive them. 10 Enter into the rock, hide in the dust, before the terror of the Lord and the splendor of his majesty. 11 The haughty eyes of mortals will be humbled and the pride of men will be humbled, and the Lord alone will be exalted on that day. 12 For the Lord of hosts will one day, against all pride and all height and against all that is exalted, in order to humble it, 13 against all the cedars of Lebanon, tall and lofty, and against all the oaks of Bashan, 14 against all the high mountains and against all the lofty hills, 15 against every magnificent tower and against every strong wall, 16 against all the ships of Tarshish and against everything that delights the eye. 17 The arrogance of mortals will be humbled and the pride of men will be brought low, and the Lord alone will be exalted on that day. 18 And all the idols will disappear. 19 And they will enter the caverns of the rocks and the caverns of the earth, before the terror of the Lord and the splendor of his majesty, when he rises to terrify the earth. 20 On that day man will throw away his silver idols and his gold idols, which he made to worship, to the rats and the bats, 21 to flee into the holes in the stone and into the clefts of the rocks, before the terror of the Lord and the splendor of his majesty, when he rises to terrify the earth. 22 Stop trusting in man, in whose nostrils there is only a breath, for what esteem can one have in him?

Isaiah 3

1 For behold, the Lord, God of hosts, is about to withdraw from Jerusalem and Judah all resources and support, all supplies of bread and all supplies of water, 2 hero and warrior, judge, prophet and seer, ancient, 3 Captain and notable, advisor, skilled craftsman and adept enchanter. 4 And I will give them young men as princes, and children will rule over them. 5 And the people will rush man against man, each against his neighbor, they will rush forward, the young man against the old man and the man of no account against the most illustrious. 6 When a man takes his brother into his father's house, saying, "You have a cloak; be our leader, and let this ruin be under your protection."« 7 On that day he will cry out, «I do not want to be a physician; I have neither bread nor a cloak in my house; do not make me a leader of the people.» 8 For Jerusalem is reeling and Judah is falling, because their words and their deeds are against the Lord, to defy the gaze of his majesty. 9 The look on their faces testifies against them; like Sodom, they proclaim their sin and do not hide it. Woe to them, for they are the authors of their own destruction. 10 Tell him that he is happy, for he will eat the fruit of his works. 11 Woe to the wicked, evil will befall him, for what his hands have done will be repaid to him. 12 My people are oppressed by children, and women rule them. My people, those who lead you astray and ruin the path you should follow. 13 The Lord has risen to render justice, and he stands to judge the peoples. 14 The Lord enters into judgment with the elders and princes of his people: «You have devoured the vine, the plunder of the poor is in your houses. 15 »By what right do you trample my people and crush the faces of the unfortunate?” declares the Lord, God of hosts., 16 The Lord said: Because the daughters of Zion have become proud, they walk with their heads held high, casting glances, they walk on small steps and jingle the rings on their feet, 17 The Lord will make the heads of the daughters of Zion bald, and the Lord will uncover their nakedness. 18 On that day, the Lord will remove the luxury of rings, suns, and crescents, 19 earrings, bracelets and veils, 20 diadems, anklets, and belts, 21 perfume boxes and amulets, rings and nose rings, 22 party dresses and wide tunics, coats and bags, 23 mirrors and muslins, turbans and mantillas. 24 And instead of perfume, there will be rot; instead of a belt, a rope; instead of curly hair, a bald head; instead of a flowing robe, a sack; instead of beauty, the mark of fire. 25 Your warriors will fall by the sword and your heroes in battle. 26 The gates of Zion will groan and mourn, and she will sit desolate in the dust.

    Isaiah 4

    1 Seven women will seize one man on that day and say, «We will eat our own bread and wear our own clothes; only allow us to bear your name, and take away our disgrace.» 2 On that day, the branch of the Lord will be the ornament and the glory and the fruit of the earth, will be the pride and the adornment of the survivors of Israel. 3 And those who remain in Zion and those who are spared in Jerusalem will be called saints, all those whose names are written for life in Jerusalem. 4 When the Lord has washed away the filthiness of the daughters of Zion and cleansed Jerusalem of the blood that is in her midst, by the spirit of judgment and by the spirit of destruction, 5 Then the Lord will create over all Mount Zion and its assemblies a cloud by day and smoke and the brightness of a blazing flame by night. For over all his glory there will be a canopy, 6 and there will be a tent to provide shade from the heat of the day and to serve as a refuge and shelter from the storm and rain.

    Isaiah 5

    1 I will sing for my beloved the song of my beloved about his vineyard. My beloved had a vineyard on a fertile hillside. 2 He dug up the soil, removed the stones, and planted it with exquisite vines. He built a tower in the middle and also dug a winepress there. He expected it to bear grapes, but it produced sour grapes. 3 «And now, you inhabitants of Jerusalem and men of Judah, judge, I pray you, between me and between my vineyard.”. 4 What more could I have done for my vineyard that I haven't already done for it? Why did I wait for it to bear grapes, and it only produced sour grapes? 5 «And now I will tell you what I am going to do to my vineyard: I will tear down its hedge and it will be grazed, I will break down its wall and it will be trampled underfoot. 6 I will make it a desert, and it will no longer be pruned or cultivated; briers and thorns will grow there, and I will command the clouds to no longer let rain fall on it.» 7 For the vineyard of the Lord of hosts is the house of Israel, and the men of Judah are the plant he cherished; he looked for justice from them, but behold, bloodshed, righteousness, and a cry of distress. 8 Woe to those who add house to house, who join field to field, until there is no more space and they dwell alone in the midst of the land. 9 The Lord of hosts spoke this word in my ears: Yes, these many houses will be deserted, large and beautiful, they will no longer have inhabitants. 10 For ten acres of vineyards will produce only one bath, a homer of seed will produce only one ephah. 11 Woe to those who run after intoxicating drinks from the morning and who, in the evening, prolong their orgy, heated by wine. 12 The harp and the lute, the tambourine, the flute and wine, these are their feasts, but they do not regard the work of the Lord and they do not see the work of his hands. 13 That is why my people will go into exile without realizing it, its nobility will become a starving troop and its multitude will wither with thirst. 14 That is why Sheol expands and opens its mouth without measure; the splendor of Zion descends into it, with its noisy and joyful multitude. 15 Mortals will be humbled, men will be brought low, and the eyes of the proud will be brought low. 16 And the Lord of hosts will appear great in judgment, and the holy God will appear holy in justice. 17 Sheep will graze as if in their pasture, and strangers will devour the devastated fields of the rich. 18 Woe to those who draw iniquity with cords of lies and sin as with the reins of a chariot. 19 They say, «Let him hurry, let him hasten his work, so that we may see it. Let him come near and carry out the decree of the Holy One of Israel, so that we may know.» 20 Woe to those who call evil good and good evil, who make darkness light and light darkness, who make sweet bitter and bitter sweet. 21 Woe to those who are wise in their own eyes and intelligent in their own minds. 22 Woe to those who are heroes for drinking wine and valiant for mixing strong liquors. 23 To those who justify the wicked for a gift and who deprive the righteous of their rights. 24 Therefore, as the tongue of fire devours the stubble and as dry grass is consumed by the flame, so their root will be like rot and their flower will be swept away like dust, because they have rejected the law of the Lord of hosts and despised the word of the Holy One of Israel. 25 Therefore the Lord's anger burned against his people; he stretched out his hand against them and struck them. The mountains trembled, their corpses lay in the middle of the roads like refuse. Yet his anger was not turned away, and his hand remained stretched out. 26 He raises a banner for the distant nations, he whistles to them from the ends of the earth. And behold, they come swift and light. 27 Not one of them is weary or faltering, not one is slumbering or sleeping; the belt around their waist is not loosened, nor the strap of their sandals broken. 28 Their arrows are sharp, their bows are all strung, the hooves of their horses are as hard as flint, the wheels of their chariots like a hurricane. 29 Their roar is that of the lion, they roar like the lion cub: it growls and seizes its prey, it carries it off and no one can snatch it away. 30 At that time, there will be a rumbling upon the people, like the rumbling of the sea. They will look upon the land and behold, darkness, anguish, and light. Then night will spread over the sky of the land.

      Isaiah 6

      1 In the year that King Uzziah died, I saw the Lord sitting on a high and lofty throne, and the train of his robe filled the temple. 2 Seraphim stood before him, each with six wings: with two they covered their faces, with two they covered their feet, and with two they flew. 3 And they cried out to one another, saying, «Holy, holy, holy is the Lord of hosts; the whole earth is full of his glory.» 4 The foundations of the doors shook at the sound of the shouting, and the house filled with smoke. 5 Then I said, «Woe to me! I am lost, for I am a man of defiled lips, and I dwell among a people of defiled lips, and my eyes have seen the King, the Lord of hosts.» 6 But one of the Seraphim flew towards me, holding in his hand a burning coal, which he had taken from the altar with tongs. 7 He touched my mouth with it and said, "See, this has touched your lips; your iniquity is taken away and your sin atoned for."« 8 And I heard the voice of the Lord saying, «Whom shall I send, and who will go for us?» And I said, «Here I am, send me.» 9 He said, «Go and tell this people: ‘Hear but do not understand, see but do not perceive.’. 10 "Harden the hearts of this people, harden their ears, and close their eyes, so that they cannot see with their eyes or hear with their ears, nor turn back and be healed."» 11 And I said, «How long, Lord?» He answered, «Until the cities are devastated and without inhabitants, until the houses are without people, and the land is ravaged and desolate, 12 until the Lord has removed the men and the land is desolate. 13 And if even a tenth of its inhabitants remain, it too will be destroyed. But, just as the sacred tree and the oak, when they are cut down, retain their stump, so the trunk of Israel will be a holy seed.»

      Isaiah 7

      1 It came to pass in the days of Ahaz, son of Jotham, son of Uzziah, king of Judah, that Rezin, king of Syria, with Phacaeus, son of Romélie, king of Israel, came up against Jerusalem to attack it, but they could not take it. 2 The news was announced to the house of David: «Syria is encamped in Ephraim.» Then the heart of the king and the heart of his people trembled as the trees of the forest tremble in the wind. 3 And the Lord said to Isaiah, «Go out to meet Ahaz, you and Shear-Jasub, your son, at the end of the canal of the upper pool, on the road to the Fuller’s Field. 4 And you will say to him: Beware, keep calm, do not be afraid and do not let your heart fail before these two smoking embers, because of the fury of Rasin and Syria and the son of Romélie. 5 Because Syria, Ephraim, and Romélie's son have plotted evil against you, saying: 6 «Let us go up against Judah, let us terrify it, let us invade it and install the son of Tabeel as king over it, 7 Thus says the Lord God: It shall not have effect, it shall not be. 8 For the head of Syria is Damascus and the head of Damascus is Rasin, and in another sixty-five years Ephraim will have ceased to be a people. 9 And the head of Ephraim is Samaria, and the head of Samaria is the son of Romélie. If you do not believe, you will not stand.» 10 The Lord spoke again to Ahaz, saying: 11 «Ask the Lord your God for a sign; ask him in the depths of Sheol or in the heights of heaven.» 12 But Ahaz said, "I will not ask, I will not test the Lord."« 13 And Isaiah said: «Listen, house of David: Is it too little for you to weary men, that you weary my God also? 14 Therefore the Lord himself will give you a sign: Behold, the Virgin has conceived and bears a son, and calls him Emmanuel. 15 He will eat cream and honey until he knows how to reject evil and choose good. 16 For before the child knows how to reject evil and choose good, the land whose two kings you fear will be devastated. 17 The Lord will bring upon you, upon your people, and upon your father’s house, days such as have not come since the day Ephraim separated from Judah, the king of Assyria.» 18 On that day the Lord will whistle for the fly that is at the end of the rivers of Egypt and for the bee that is in the land of Assyria. 19 They will come and settle all of them in the steep valleys and in the crevices of the rocks, on all the bushes and on all the pastures. 20 On that day the Lord will shave with a razor that he will have hired beyond the River with the king of Assyria, the head and the hair of the feet, and he will also remove the beard. 21 On that day, a man will feed one cow and two sheep 22 and, because of the abundance of milk they will give, they will eat nothing but cream, for it is cream and honey that all those who remain in the country will eat. 23 On that day, every place where there were a thousand vines, worth a thousand pieces of silver, will be covered with thorns and briers. 24 They will enter it with arrows and with bows, for the whole land will be full of thorns and briers. 25 And on all the mountains that were cultivated with the hoe, you will no longer go, for fear of brambles and thorns; they will become a pasture for cattle and a land trampled by sheep.

      Isaiah 8

      1 And the Lord said to me, «Take a large tablet and write on it in characters that all can read: Hasten the plunder. As the first time brought shame upon the land of Zebulun and the land of Naphtali, so the last time will bring glory to the Way of the Sea, to the land beyond the Jordan, and to the district of the nations.

      Isaiah 9

      1 The people who walked in darkness have seen a great light, and on those who dwelt in the land of the shadow of death, a light has dawned. 2 You have multiplied your people, you have made their joy great; they rejoice before you as people rejoice at the harvest, as people shout when dividing the spoils. 3 For the yoke that weighed upon him, the bar that struck his shoulder, the staff of his oppressor, you have broken as on the day of Midian. 4 For every sandal of the warrior in the melee and every cloak rolled in blood are given over to the fire, the fire devours them. 5 For to us a child is born, to us a son is given; and the government is placed upon his shoulders, and his name is called Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace: 6 To extend the empire and to give endless peace to the throne of David and to his kingship, to establish and uphold it in justice and righteousness, now and forever. The zeal of the Lord of hosts will accomplish this. 7 The Lord sent a word to Jacob, and it fell upon Israel., 8 All the people will know about it, Ephraim and the inhabitants of Samaria, who say in their pride and in the pride of their hearts: 9 «"The bricks have fallen, let us build with hewn stone; the sycamores are cut down, let us replace them with cedars."» 10 The Lord raises up against them the adversaries of Rasin and he goads their enemies., 11 Syria to the east, the Philistines to the west, and they will devour Israel with gusto. Despite all this, his anger has not turned away, and his hand remains outstretched. 12 For the people did not return to the One who struck them, nor did they seek the Lord of hosts. 13 And the Lord will cut off from Israel the head and the tail, the palm and the rush, in a single day. 14 The ancient and noble man is the head, and the prophet who teaches lies is the tail. 15 Those who guide this people lead them astray, and those who are guided are lost. 16 Therefore the Lord will not be pleased with his young men, nor will he have compassion on his fatherless children and widows, for they are all ungodly and wicked, and every mouth speaks ungodliness. For all this his anger has not turned away, and his hand remains stretched out. 17 For malice has been kindled like a fire, it devours brambles and thorns, it sets the thickets of the forest ablaze, and the smoke rises in swirling eddies. 18 By the wrath of the Lord of hosts, the land is ablaze and the people have become prey to the flames.       Isaiah 10

      1 Woe to those who pronounce unjust judgments and to the scribes who write unjust sentences, 2 excluding the weak from the court and depriving the afflicted of my people of their rights, making widows their prey and plundering orphans. 3 What will you do on the day of the visitation and in the catastrophe that will come from afar? To whom will you flee for help and where will you deposit your treasures? 4 All that remains is to bow down among the captives, or to fall among the slain.                                                                       Isaiah 65

Do not come near me, for I am holy to you.» Those are like smoke in my nostrils, a fire that burns continually. 6 Behold, it is written before me: I will not remain silent until I have repaid, repaid within their midst 7 your iniquities, with the iniquities of your fathers, says the Lord, who burned incense on the mountains and insulted me on the hills, I will measure out to them in their bosom the wages of their former conduct. 8 Thus says the Lord: Just as, finding juice in a bunch of grapes, one says, «Do not destroy it, for there is a blessing in it,» so shall I act for the sake of my servants, so that I will not destroy everything. 9 I will bring forth offspring from Jacob and from Judah an heir of my mountains; my chosen ones will possess them, and my servants will dwell there. 10 And Sharon will serve as a sheepfold and the valley of Achor as a pasture for cattle, for my people who have sought me. 11 But you who have forsaken the Lord, forgotten my holy mountain, who set a table for Gad and fill a bowl for Meni, 12 I destine you for the sword, and you will all bow down to be slaughtered. For I called, and you did not answer; I spoke, and you did not listen, but you did what was evil in my sight and chose what I did not want. 13 Therefore this is what the Lord God says: “My servants will eat, but you will go hungry; my servants will drink, but you will go thirsty; my servants will rejoice, but you will be ashamed.”, 14 Behold, my servants will sing with joy in their hearts, and you will cry out in the pain of your hearts and howl in the anguish of your spirits., 15 And you will leave your name as a curse to my chosen ones, and the Lord God will cause you to perish, but he will call his servants by another name. 16 Whoever wishes to be blessed on earth will wish to be blessed by the God of truth, and whoever swears an oath on earth will swear by the God of truth. For the previous anxieties will be forgotten and will have vanished from my sight. 17 For behold, I create new heavens and a new earth; the former things shall not be remembered, nor shall they come to mind. 18 Rather, rejoice and be ever glad in what I am creating: for behold, I am creating Jerusalem for joy and its people for gladness. 19 And I will rejoice over Jerusalem and be glad over my people. And the sound of weeping and the cry of anguish will no longer be heard in it. 20 There will no longer be any child born for a few days, nor any old man who does not fulfill the number of his days, for to die a hundred years old will be to die young, and it is at a hundred years that the curse will reach the sinner. 21 They will build houses and live in them, they will plant vineyards and eat their fruit. 22 They will not build for another to inhabit, nor will they plant for another to eat. For the days of my people will be like the days of the trees, and my chosen ones will wear out the work of their hands. 23 They will no longer labor in vain, nor will they bear children for sudden death, for they will be a race blessed by the Lord, and with them their offspring. 24 Before they call, I will answer; they will speak again, and I will grant their wish. 25 The wolf and the lamb will graze together, the serpent will feed on dust, the lion will eat straw like the ox, and the serpent will feed on dirt. They will neither harm nor destroy on all my holy mountain, says the Lord.

                                                                      Isaiah 66

                                                                      1 Thus says the Lord: Heaven is my throne and the earth is my footstool: what kind of house would you build for me, and what would be the place of my rest? 2 All these things my hand made, and all these things came into being, declares the Lord. This is the one I look upon: the humble, the brokenhearted, and he trembles at my word. 3 He who slaughters an ox kills a man, he who sacrifices a sheep slaughters a dog, he who presents a grain offering offers pig's blood, he who burns incense blesses an idol. They choose their ways and their souls delight in their abominations, 4 I too will choose their misfortune and bring upon them what they fear, because I called and no one answered, I spoke and they did not listen, they did what was evil in my sight and they chose what displeased me. 5 Listen to the word of the Lord, you who tremble at his word: Your brothers who hate you and reject you because of my name have said, «Let the Lord show his glory, so that we may see your joy.» But they will be put to shame. 6 A crash, a tumult rises from the city, a shout from the temple. It is the voice of the Lord, who repays his enemies what they deserve. 7 Before she went into labor, she gave birth; before the pains came to her, she brought forth a male child. 8 Who has ever heard of such a thing, who has ever seen anything like it? Is a country born in a day, is a nation brought forth in a single moment, that Zion, scarcely in labor, has given birth to her children? 9 Shall I open the womb and not bring forth? says the Lord, or shall I, who bring forth, close the womb? says your God. 10 Rejoice with Jerusalem and be glad for her, all you who love her. Rejoice greatly with her, all you who mourned over her, 11 so that you may be nursed and filled with his comforts, so that you may enjoy with delight the fullness of his glory. 12 For thus says the Lord: I will cause peace to flow upon her like a river and the glory of the nations like an overflowing stream; and you shall be nursed, carried on the breast, and caressed on the knees. 13 As a man whom his mother comforts, so I will comfort you, and you will be comforted in Jerusalem. 14 You will see it, and your heart will rejoice, and your bones will flourish like grass. And the hand of the Lord will be made known to his servants, and his wrath to his enemies. 15 For behold, the Lord is coming in fire, and his chariot is like a hurricane, to pour out his wrath in blazing flames and his threats in flames of fire. 16 For the Lord executes judgment by fire and by his sword against all flesh, and many will be those whom the Lord will pierce. 17 Those who consecrate and purify themselves to go into the gardens, following the one who stands in the middle, those who eat pork, abominable foods, and mice, will all perish together, declares the Lord., 18 I know their works and their thoughts. The time has come to gather all nations and all languages. They will come and see my glory, 19 and I will perform a wonder among them. And I will send some of their survivors to the nations, to Tarshish, to Phul and Lud, who draw the bow, to Tubal and Javan, to the distant islands, who have never heard of me or seen my glory, and they will proclaim my glory among the nations. 20 And they shall bring all your brothers from among all the nations, as an offering to the Lord, on horses, on chariots, in litters, on mules and camels, to my holy mountain, to Jerusalem, says the Lord, as the children of Israel bring the offering in a pure vessel to the house of the Lord. 21 And I will even take some of them as priests and Levites, says the Lord. 22 For as the new heavens and the new earth, which I am going to create, will remain before me, declares the Lord, so will your offspring and your name remain. 23 From new moon to new moon and from sabbath to sabbath, all flesh shall come to bow down before me, says the Lord. 24 And when they go out, they will see the corpses of the men who rebelled against me, for their worm will not die and their fire will not be quenched, and they will be an abomination to all flesh.
