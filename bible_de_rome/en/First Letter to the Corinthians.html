1 Corinthians 1

1 Paul, an apostle of Jesus Christ called by the will of God, and Sosthenes, his brother, 2 to the Church of God which is in Corinth, to the faithful sanctified in Jesus Christ, saints by vocation, and to all those who invoke, in any place whatsoever, the name of Our Lord Jesus Christ, their Lord and ours. 3 Grace and peace be given to you from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ. 4 I continually give thanks to my God for you, for the grace of God which was given you in Christ Jesus. 5 For through your union with him you have been enriched in every way, in all speech and in all knowledge, 6 the testimony of Christ having been firmly established among you, 7 so that you do not give it up to anyone in any gift of grace, waiting with confidence for the revelation of Our Lord Jesus Christ. 8 He will also keep you firm to the end, so that you will be blameless on the day of our Lord Jesus Christ. 9 He is faithful, the God who has called you into fellowship with his Son Jesus Christ, our Lord. 10 I urge you, brothers, in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ, that all of you speak with one accord, that there be no division among you, but that you be perfectly united in the same mind and the same thought. 11 For it has been reported to me concerning you, my brothers, by Chloe's people, that there are quarrels among you. 12 I mean that one of you says, "I follow Paul," another, "I follow Apollos," and another, "I follow Cephas," and another, "I follow Christ."« 13 Is Christ divided? Was Paul crucified for you? Were you baptized in the name of Paul? 14 I thank God that I baptized none of you except Crispus and Gaius, 15 so that no one can say that he was baptized in my name. 16 I also baptized Stephanas' family; besides, I don't know that I baptized anyone else. 17 Christ did not send me to baptize, but to preach the gospel, not with wise words, so that the cross of Christ might not be emptied of its power. 18 Indeed, the doctrine of the cross is foolishness to those who are perishing, but to us who are being saved it is divine power. 19 For it is written: «I will destroy the wisdom of the wise, and I will annihilate the knowledge of the learned.» 20 Where is the wise man? Where is the doctor? Where is the debater of this world? Has not God made foolish the wisdom of the world? 21 For since in the wisdom of God the world through its wisdom did not know God, God was pleased through the foolishness of what was preached to save those who believe. 22 The Jews demand miracles and the Greeks seek wisdom., 23 We preach Christ crucified, a stumbling block to Jews and folly to Gentiles, 24 But to those who are called, whether Jews or Greeks, the power of God and the wisdom of God. 25 For what is foolishness in God is wiser than the wisdom of men, and what is weakness in God is stronger than the strength of men. 26 Consider your calling, my brothers: not many of you are wise according to worldly standards, not many are powerful, not many are of noble birth. 27 But what the world considers foolish is what God has chosen to confound the wise, and what the world considers worthless is what God has chosen to confound the strong., 28 And God chose the things in the world that are without regard and power, and that are nothing, to nullify the things that are., 29 so that no flesh may boast before God. 30 But it is through him that you are in Christ Jesus, who has become for us wisdom and righteousness and sanctification and redemption from God. 31 so that, according to the word of Scripture, "he who boasts, boasts in the Lord."«

1 Corinthians 2

1 My brothers, when I came to you, I did not come with eloquence or wisdom to proclaim to you the testimony of God. 2 For I did not decide that I should know anything while I was with you except Jesus Christ and him crucified. 3 But it was in weakness, in fear, and in great trembling that I came to you, 4 And my words and my preaching had nothing of the persuasive language of wisdom, but the Holy Spirit and the power of God demonstrated their truth. 5 so that your faith may rest not on human wisdom, but on God's power. 6 Yet there is a wisdom that we preach among the perfect, a wisdom that is not of this world, nor of the princes of this world, whose reign is coming to an end. 7 We preach a mysterious and hidden wisdom of God, which God, before the ages, had destined for our glorification.. 8 This wisdom, none of the princes of this world understood, for if they had understood it, they would not have crucified the Lord of glory. 9 But these are, as it is written, «things which no eye has seen, nor ear heard, nor have entered into the mind of man, things which God has prepared for those who love him.» 10 It is to us that God has revealed them through his Spirit, for the Spirit penetrates all things, even the deep things of God. 11 For who among men knows what is happening in a man except the man's spirit within him? In the same way no one knows what is happening in God except the Spirit of God. 12 For we have received not the spirit of the world, but the Spirit who is from God, so that we may understand the things which God has given us by his grace. 13 And we speak of these things, not in words taught by human wisdom, but in words taught by the Spirit, expressing spiritual things in spiritual language. 14But the natural man does not receive the things of the Spirit of God, for they are foolishness to him, and he is not able to understand them, because they are discerned by the Spirit. 15 The spiritual man, on the contrary, judges everything, and is himself judged by no one. 16 For "who has known the mind of the Lord, so as to instruct him?" But we have the mind of Christ.

1 Corinthians 3

1Brothers, I could not speak to you as to spiritual men, but as to carnal men, as to little children in Christ. 2 I gave you milk to drink, not solid food, for you were not yet able to eat, and you are not yet able, because you are still carnal. 3 For since there is jealousy and quarreling among you, are you not carnal and do you not walk according to man? 4 When one says, "I belong to Paul," and another, "I belong to Apollos," are you not men? 5 What then is Apollos? And what is Paul? Servants through whom you believed, according to what the Lord assigned to each. 6 I planted, Apollos watered, but God made it grow. 7 Thus neither the one who plants is anything, nor the one who waters, but God, who makes things grow. 8 The one who plants and the one who waters are equal, and each will receive their own reward according to their own work. 9 For we are God's fellow workers. You are God's field, God's building. 10 According to the grace of God given to me, I, like a wise master builder, laid a foundation, and another builds upon it. Only let each one take care how he builds upon it. 11 For no one can lay any other foundation than the one already laid, which is Jesus Christ. 12 If one builds on this foundation with gold, silver, precious stones, wood, hay, straw, 13 The work of each one will be revealed, for the day of the Lord will make it known, because it will be revealed in fire, and the fire itself will test what the work of each one is. 14 If the structure built upon it survives, a reward will be received., 15 If someone's work is consumed, he will lose his reward, yet he himself will be saved, but only as through fire. 16 Do you not know that you are a temple of God and that the Spirit of God dwells in you? 17 If anyone destroys God’s temple, God will destroy him, for God’s temple is holy, and that is what you yourselves are. 18 Let no one deceive himself. If any of you thinks he is wise in this world, let him become a fool, so that he may become wise. 19 Indeed, the wisdom of this world is foolishness in God's sight, for it is written: "I will catch the wise in their craftiness."« 20 And again: "The Lord knows the thoughts of the wise, that they are futile."« 21 Therefore, let no one boast in men, for everything is yours., 22 and Paul and Apollos and Cephas and the world and life and death and things present and things to come. 23 Everything is yours, but you belong to Christ and Christ belongs to God.

1 Corinthians 4

1 Therefore, let us be regarded as servants of Christ and dispensers of the mysteries of God. 2 Well, what we look for in providers is that each one be found faithful. 3 For me, it matters very little whether I am judged by you or by a human tribunal; I do not judge myself., 4 For although I feel guilty of nothing, I am not thereby justified: my judge is the Lord. 5 Therefore do not judge anything before the appointed time, until the Lord comes: he will bring to light what is hidden in darkness and will expose the motives of the hearts, and then each will receive from God the praise that is due them. 6 What I have just said about Apollos and myself is only a form I have used for your benefit, brothers, so that you may learn from us not to go beyond what is written, not to be puffed up with pride in favor of one against another. 7 For who makes you different from anyone else? What do you have that you did not receive? And if you did receive it, why do you boast as though you did not? 8 You are already full. You are already rich. Without us, you are kings. God grant that you truly are kings, so that we too may reign with you. 9 For it seems that God has made us, the Apostles, appear as the last of men, as those condemned to death, because we have been made a spectacle to the world, to angels, and to men. 10 We are fools for Christ’s sake, but you are wise in Christ Jesus; we are weak, but you are strong; you are honored, but we are despised. 11 Even now, we suffer from hunger, thirst, and nakedness; we are bruised and battered; we have neither fire nor shelter., 12 And we toil with our own hands; cursed, we bless; persecuted, we endure. 13 Slandered, we beg, we are thus far like the world's scum, the refuse of men. 14 I am not writing these things to shame you, but to warn you as my beloved children. 15 For even if you had ten thousand teachers in Christ, you do not have many fathers, since I became your father in Christ Jesus through the gospel. 16 I therefore urge you, be imitators of me, as I am of Christ. 17 That is why I have sent you Timothy, who is my beloved and faithful child in the Lord, he will remind you of my ways in Christ Jesus, how I teach everywhere, in all the churches. 18 Some, assuming that I would no longer come to you, became puffed up with pride. 19 But I will come to you soon, if the Lord pleases, and I will find out not the words of those who are arrogant, but what they can do. 20 For the kingdom of God consists not in words, but in deeds. 21 What do you want? For me to come to you with a stick, or with love and in a spirit of gentleness?

    1 Corinthians 5

    1 We hear of nothing but lewdness among you, and of such lewdness that even pagans do not find anything like it, to the point that a man is sleeping with his father’s wife. 2 And you are puffed up with pride. And you did not rather mourn, so that he who committed such an act might be cut off from among you. 3 For me, absent in body but present in spirit, I have already judged, as if I were present, the one who committed such an act. 4 In the name of Our Lord Jesus Christ, all of you gathered here, and I in spirit among you, with the power of Our Lord Jesus, 5 that such a man be handed over to Satan for the death of the flesh, so that his spirit may be saved on the day of the Lord Jesus. 6 You are wrong to boast so much. Don't you know that a little leaven makes the whole dough rise? 7 Cleanse yourselves of the old leaven, so that you may be a new batch of dough, just as you are unleavened bread. For Christ, our Passover lamb, has been sacrificed. 8 Let us therefore celebrate the festival, not with old leaven nor with the leaven of malice and perversity, but with the unleavened bread of purity and truth. 9 I wrote to you in my letter not to have relations with immodest people, 10 not at all with the immoral of this world, or with greedy and rapacious men, or with idolaters, otherwise you would have to leave the world. 11 I simply wanted to tell you not to associate with anyone who claims to be a brother but is sexually immoral or greedy, an idolater, a slanderer, a drunkard, or a predator; not even to eat with such a person. 12 For is it for me to judge those outside? Is it not for you to judge those inside? 13 Those outside the church are judged by God. Expel the wicked from among you.

    1 Corinthians 6

    1 When one of you has a dispute with another, does he dare to go to court before the unjust and not before the saints? 2 Do you not know that the saints will judge the world? And if the world is to be judged by you, are you unworthy to judge lesser matters? 3 Do you not know that we will judge the angels? Why not, all the more so, the affairs of this life? 4 Therefore, when you have judgments to be rendered concerning the matters of this life, you appoint to judge them those who are least esteemed in the Church! 5 I say this to your shame: so there is not a wise man among you, not one who can give a judgment between his brothers. 6 But a brother is in litigation with another brother, and this is happening in front of infidels. 7 It is certainly already a fault of yours to be having lawsuits with one another. Why not suffer some injustice instead? Why not let yourselves be robbed instead? 8 But it is you yourselves who commit injustice and plunder others, and it is your brothers. 9 Do you not know that the unrighteous will not inherit the kingdom of God? Do not be deceived: neither the sexually immoral, nor idolaters, nor adulterers, nor men who practice homosexuality, nor thieves, 10 Neither thieves, nor the greedy, nor drunkards, nor slanderers, nor predators will possess the kingdom of God. 11 Yet that is what you were, or at least some of you were, but you were washed, you were sanctified, you were justified in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ and by the Spirit of our God. 12 Everything is permitted to me, but not everything is useful; everything is permitted to me, but I will not allow myself to be dominated by anything. 13 Food is for the stomach and the stomach for food, and God will destroy both. But the body is not meant for sexual immorality, but for the Lord, and the Lord for the body. 14 And God, who raised the Lord from the dead, will also raise us up by his power. 15 Do you not know that your bodies are members of Christ? Shall I then take the members of Christ and make them members of a prostitute? Far from it. 16 Do you not know that he who unites himself with a prostitute becomes one body with her? For, as Scripture says, «They will both become one flesh.» 17 On the contrary, he who unites himself with the Lord is one spirit with him. 18 Flee from sexual immorality. Every other sin a person commits is outside the body, but whoever commits sexual immorality sins against their own body. 19 Do you not know that your body is the temple of the Holy Spirit who is in you, whom you have received from God, and that you are no longer your own? 20 For you were bought at a price. Therefore glorify God in your body.

    1 Corinthians 7

    1 Regarding the points you wrote to me about, I will tell you that it is good for a man not to touch a woman. 2 However, to avoid any immorality, each man should have his wife and each woman her husband. 3 The husband should render to his wife what he owes her, and the wife should do the same to her husband. 4The woman cannot do what she wants with her own body, but the husband, likewise the husband cannot do what he wants with his body, but the wife. 5 Do not separate from one another except perhaps by agreement for a time, to devote yourselves to prayer; then come together again, lest Satan tempt you because of your lack of self-control. 6 I say this condescendingly, I am not making it an order. 7 On the contrary, I wish that all men were like me, but each one receives from God his particular gift, one in one way, another in another. 8 To the unmarried and the widows I say that it is good for them to remain as I am. 9 But if they cannot restrain themselves, let them marry, for it is better to marry than to burn. 10 As for married people, I command, not I, but the Lord, that a wife must not separate from her husband., 11 If she is separated from him, let her remain unmarried or be reconciled to her husband; likewise, let the husband not divorce his wife. 12 To the others I say, I (not the Lord): If any brother has a wife who does not have faith, and she is willing to live with him, he must not send her away., 13 And if a woman has a husband who does not have faith, and he consents to live with her, she should not divorce him. 14 For the unfaithful husband is sanctified through the wife and the unfaithful wife is sanctified through the husband; otherwise your children would be unclean, but as it is, they are holy. 15 If the unbeliever separates, let him separate; the brother or sister is not enslaved under these conditions. God has called us in peace. 16 For how do you know, wife, whether you will save your husband? Or how do you know, husband, whether you will save your wife? 17 However, let each one conduct himself according to the position that the Lord has assigned him and according to the calling that God has given him; this is the rule that I establish in all the churches. 18 Anyone who was called while circumcised should not conceal their circumcision; anyone who was called while uncircumcised should not be circumcised. 19 Circumcision is nothing, uncircumcision is nothing; what matters is observing God's commandments. 20 Let each one remain in the state in which he was called. 21 Were you called while you were a slave? Do not be troubled by it; but even though you could become free, make the most of your calling. 22 For the slave who was called in the Lord is the Lord’s freedman, so the free man who was called is Christ’s slave. 23 You were bought at a great price; do not make yourselves slaves to men. 24 Let each one, brothers, remain before God in the state he was in when he was called. 25 As for virgins, I have no commandment from the Lord, but I give advice, as one who has received grace from the Lord to be faithful. 26 Therefore, because of the present difficulties, I think it is good for a man to be this way. 27 If you are bound to a woman, do not seek to break that bond; if you are not bound to a woman, do not seek a woman. 28 But if you married, you did not sin, and if a virgin married, she did not sin; but these people will suffer trouble in the flesh, and I want to spare you from that. 29 But here is what I say, brothers: the time is short, therefore those who have wives should live as if they do not., 30 those who weep as if they do not weep, those who rejoice as if they do not rejoice, those who buy as if they do not own, 31 and those who use the world as if not using it, for the form of this world is passing away. 32 But I would like you to be free from concern. An unmarried man is concerned about the things of the Lord, he seeks to please the Lord, 33 A married man is concerned with worldly things; he seeks to please his wife., 34 and he is divided. Likewise, the wife, the unmarried woman, and the virgin are concerned about the things of the Lord, that they may be holy in body and spirit; but the married woman is concerned about the things of the world, she seeks to please her husband. 35 I say this for your benefit, not to cast a net over you, but for what is proper and suitable for uniting you to the Lord without conflict. 36 If someone judges that he would expose his daughter to dishonor, if she is past the prime of life and it is his duty to marry her off, let him do as he wishes, he does not sin, let her marry. 37 But he who, without being forced to do so, being free to do what he wants, has put a firm resolution in his heart and decided to keep his daughter a virgin, he does well. 38 Thus, he who marries off his daughter does well, and he who does not marry her off does better. 39 The wife is bound as long as her husband lives; if the husband dies, she is free to remarry whomever she wishes, only that it be in the Lord. 40 She is happier, however, if she remains as she is: that is my opinion and I believe I too have the Spirit of God.

      1 Corinthians 8

      1 As for meat sacrificed to idols, we know, for we are all enlightened. Knowledge puffs up, but charity builds up. 2 If anyone presumes to know, he has not yet known anything as he ought to know. 3 But if anyone loves God, he is known by him. 4 As for eating meat sacrificed to idols, we know that an idol is nothing in the world and that there is only one God. 5 For if there are beings who are called gods, whether in heaven or on earth, then there are many gods and many lords, 6 For us, however, there is but one God, the Father, from whom all things came and for whom we exist; and one Lord, Jesus Christ, through whom all things came and through whom we exist. 7 But not all have this knowledge. Some, still retaining their old way of viewing the idol, eat these meats as if they had been sacrificed to an idol, and their conscience, which is weak, is defiled. 8 Food is not something that commends us to God; if we eat it, we have nothing more, if we do not eat it, we have nothing less. 9 However, beware that this freedom you enjoy does not become an occasion of stumbling for the weak. 10 For if someone sees you, a man of understanding, sitting at a table in an idol's temple, will not his conscience, being weak, lead him to eat food sacrificed to idols? 11 And so the weak are lost by your knowledge, this brother for whom Christ died. 12 By sinning in this way against your brothers and violating their still weak conscience, you sin against Christ. 13 Therefore, if anything causes my brother to stumble over anything that causes him to stumble, I will abstain from meat forever, so that I will not cause him to stumble.

        1 Corinthians 9

        1 Am I not free? Am I not an apostle? Have I not seen Jesus our Lord? Are you not my workmanship in the Lord? 2 If I am not an apostle to others, I am at least one to you, for you are the seal of my apostleship in the Lord. 3 That's my answer to my detractors. 4 Don't we have the right to eat and drink? 5 Do we not have the right to bring a sister with us, as the other Apostles and the brothers of the Lord and Cephas do? 6 Or are Barnabé and I the only ones who don't have the right not to work? 7Who has ever borne arms at his own expense? Who plants a vineyard and will not eat its fruit? Who tends a flock, and does not feed on its milk? 8 Am I saying these things according to human reason, and does not the Law also say them? 9 For it is written in the Law of Moses: «You shall not muzzle the mouth of an ox while it is treading out the grain.» Does God care about oxen? 10 Is it not entirely for our sake that he speaks thus? Yes, it was written for our sake: that the one who plows should plow in hope, and that the one who threshes should thresh in the hope of sharing in the harvest. 11 If we have sown spiritual gifts among you, is it so great a matter that we reap material gifts from you? 12 If others are exercising this right over you, why not us instead? However, we have not exercised this right, but we endure everything so as not to put any obstacle in the way of the gospel of Christ. 13 Do you not know that those who perform sacred functions receive their food from the temple, and that those who serve at the altar share in what is offered at the altar? 14 Likewise, the Lord has commanded those who proclaim the Gospel to live by the Gospel. 15 For my part, I have not asserted any of these rights and it is not in order to claim them in my favor that I write this: it would be better for me to die than to let myself be deprived of this title of glory. 16 If I preach the gospel, it is not for my glory, it is an obligation placed upon me, and woe to me if I do not preach the gospel. 17 If I did it of my own free will, I would deserve a reward, but I do it by order, so it is a responsibility entrusted to me. 18 What then is my reward? It is that, in preaching the Gospel, I offer it freely, without exercising my right as a preacher of the Gospel. 19 For although I am free from all, I have made myself a servant to all, in order to win more of them. 20 To the Jews I became like a Jew, in order to win the Jews, 21 with those who are under the Law, as though I were under the Law, though I am not subject to the Law, in order to win those who are under the Law, with those who are not under the Law, as though I were not without the Law of God, being under the law of Christ, in order to win those who are not under the Law. 22 I made myself weak with the weak, in order to win over the weak. I became all things to all people in order to save some at all costs. 23 I do everything for the sake of the Gospel, so that I may share in its blessings. 24 Don't you know? In the races, everyone runs, but only one gets the prize. Run likewise, so that you may win it. 25 Whoever wants to compete must abstain from everything; they for a perishable crown, we for an imperishable one. 26 For me, I run in the same way, not as if on an adventure, I strike, not as if beating the air. 27 But I discipline my body and keep it in bondage, lest after preaching to others I myself should be disqualified.

        1 Corinthians 10

        1 For I do not want you to be unaware, brothers, that our fathers were all under the cloud, that they all passed through the sea, 2 and that they were all baptized into Moses in the cloud and in the sea, 3that they all ate the same spiritual food, 4 and that they all drank the same spiritual drink, for they drank from a spiritual rock that accompanied them, and that rock was Christ. 5 However, God did not find pleasure in most of them, since their bodies were scattered in the desert. 6 These things were symbols of what is happening to us, so that we might not have sinful desires, as they did. 7and that you do not become idolaters, as some of them were, as it is written: «The people sat down to eat and drink, and rose up to play.» 8 Let us not indulge in sexual immorality, as some of them did, and in one day twenty-three thousand of them fell. 9 Let us not put Christ to the test, as some of them did, and were killed by serpents. 10 Do not murmur as some of them did, who perished under the blows of the Exterminator. 11 All these things happened to them in symbolism, and they were written for our instruction, on whom the ends of the ages have come. 12 Therefore, let him who thinks he is standing take heed lest he fall. 13 No temptation has overtaken you except what is common to mankind. And God is faithful; he will not let you be tempted beyond what you can bear. But when you are tempted, he will also provide a way out by giving you the power to endure it. 14 Therefore, my beloved, flee from idolatry. 15 I am speaking to you as to intelligent men; judge for yourselves what I say. 16 The cup of blessing, which we bless, is it not a communion in the blood of Christ? And the bread, which we break, is it not a communion in the body of Christ? 17 Since there is only one loaf, we form one body, while being many, for we all partake of the same loaf. 18 Consider Israel according to the flesh: do not those who eat the sacrifices partake of the altar? 19 What does this mean? That meat sacrificed to idols is something, or that an idol is something? 20 Not at all, I am saying that what the pagans offer as a sacrifice they offer to demons and not to God, and I do not want you to be in communion with demons. 21 You cannot drink from both the cup of the Lord and the cup of demons; you cannot partake of the table of the Lord and the table of demons. 22 Do we want to provoke the Lord's jealousy? Are we stronger than him? 23 Everything is permitted, but not everything is beneficial; everything is permitted, but not everything is edifying. 24 Let no one seek their own advantage, but that of others. 25 Eat everything that is sold in the market, without questioning it on grounds of conscience., 26 for "the earth belongs to the Lord, and everything in it."« 27 If an unbeliever invites you and you want to go, eat whatever is put before you, without raising any questions on account of conscience. 28 But if someone tells you, "This has been offered as a sacrifice," do not eat it, both because of the one who told you this and because of your conscience. 29 I am talking about conscience, not yours, but that of another. Why, indeed, should my freedom be judged by a foreign conscience? 30 If I eat with thanksgiving, why should I be blamed for something for which I give thanks? 31 So whether you eat or drink, or whatever you do, do it all for the glory of God. 32 Do not cause offense to Jews, Greeks, or the Church of God. 33 In this way I myself strive in all things to please everyone, not seeking my own advantage, but that of the many, so that they may be saved.

        1 Corinthians 11

        1 Be imitators of me, as I am of Christ. 2 I commend you for remembering me in every respect and for heeding my instructions just as I gave them to you. 3 However, I want you to know that the head of every man is Christ, the head of a woman is man, and the head of Christ is God. 4 Any man who prays or prophesies with his head covered dishonors his head. 5 Every woman who prays or prophesies with her head uncovered dishonors her head; she is like one whose head is shaved. 6 If a woman does not cover her head, she should have her hair cut off. But if it is a disgrace for a woman to have her hair cut off or her head shaved, then she should cover her head. 7 A man should not cover his head, because he is the image of God’s glory, while woman is the glory of man. 8 Indeed, man did not come from woman, but woman from man. 9 And man was not created for woman, but woman for man. 10 That is why a woman must, because of the angels, have a sign of submission on her head. 11 However, neither woman is without man, nor man without woman, in the Lord. 12 For just as woman came from man, so also man is born of woman, and everything comes from God. 13 Judge for yourselves: is it proper for a woman to pray to God without being veiled? 14 Does not nature itself teach us that it is a shame for a man to wear long hair?, 15 Whereas long hair is a glory for a woman, because hair was given to her as a veil? 16 If anyone likes to dispute, we do not have that custom, nor do the Churches of God. 17 But in recommending this point to you, I am not praising you for assembling yourselves, not for your benefit, but for your harm. 18 And first, I learn that when you meet in assembly, there are divisions among you, and I partly believe it, 19 For there must be factions among you, so that the true brothers may be revealed among you, 20 Therefore, when you gather together, it is no longer the Lord's Supper that you are celebrating., 21 because, at the table, each person begins by eating their own meal, so that some are hungry, while others gorge themselves. 22 Do you not have houses to eat and drink in? Or do you despise the church of God and want to insult those who have nothing? What shall I say to you? That I praise you? No, I do not praise you in this. 23 For I received from the Lord what I also passed on to you: that the Lord Jesus, on the night he was betrayed, took bread, 24 And after giving thanks, he broke it and said, «[Take and eat], this is my body, [which will be given] for you; do this in remembrance of me.» 25 Likewise, after supper, he took the cup and said, «This cup is the new covenant in my blood; do this, whenever you drink it, in remembrance of me.» 26 For whenever you eat this bread and drink this cup, you proclaim the Lord’s death until he comes. 27 Therefore, whoever eats the bread or drinks the cup of the Lord unworthily will be guilty of sinning against the body and blood of the Lord. 28 Let each one therefore examine himself, and thus eat of this bread and drink of this cup, 29 for he who eats and drinks [unworthily], without discerning the body of the Lord, eats and drinks his own judgment. 30 That is why many among you are weak and sick, and a number of you have died. 31 If we examined ourselves, we would not be judged. 32 But the Lord judges and chastises us, so that we will not be condemned with this world. 33 Therefore, my brothers, when you come together to eat, wait for one another. 34 If anyone is hungry, let him eat at home, so that you will not come together for your condemnation. I will arrange the other matters when I come to you.

          1 Corinthians 12

          1 As for spiritual gifts, I do not want you to be ignorant, my brothers. 2 You know that when you were pagans, you were led astray to mute idols, as you were led there. 3Therefore I declare to you that no one speaking by the Spirit of God ever says, «Jesus is accursed,» and no one can say, “Jesus is Lord,” except by the Holy Spirit. 4 There is, however, a diversity of gifts, but it is the same Spirit. 5 Diversity of ministries, but it is the same Lord. 6 Diversity of operations, but it is the same God who works all in all. 7 To each is given the manifestation of the Spirit for the common good. 8 For to one is given through the Spirit a message of wisdom, and to another a message of knowledge, according to the same Spirit, 9 to another, faith by the same Spirit, to another, the gift of healing by that one and the same Spirit, 10 To another, the power to perform miracles, to another prophecy, to another the ability to distinguish between spirits, to another the ability to speak in different kinds of tongues, and to another the gift of interpreting them. 11 But it is the same Spirit who produces all these gifts, distributing them to each one individually, as he pleases. 12 For just as the body is one and has many members, and all the members of the body, though many, are one body, so it is with Christ. 13 For we were all baptized by one Spirit into one body—whether Jews or Greeks, slave or free—and we were all given the one Spirit to drink. 14 Thus the body is not a single limb, but is made up of several. 15 If the foot were to say, "Since I am not a hand, I am not of the body," would it be any less of the body for that reason? 16 And if the ear were to say, "Since I am not an eye, I am not of the body," would it be any less of the body for that reason? 17 If the whole body were an eye, where would hearing be? If it were entirely hearing, where would smell be? 18 But God placed each of the members in the body, just as he wanted. 19 If they were all one single member, where would the body be? 20 Therefore, there are several members and only one body. 21 The eye cannot say to the hand, "I don't need you," nor can the head say to the feet, "I don't need you."« 22 On the contrary, the body parts that appear weakest are the most necessary., 23 And those whom we consider the least honorable members of the body are those whom we treat with the most honor. Thus, we treat our least honest members with greater decency., 24 while our honest parts do not need it. God has arranged the body in such a way as to give more respect to what is less worthy, 25 so that there may be no division in the body, but that its members may have equal concern for one another. 26 And if one member suffers, all the members suffer with him; if one member is honored, all the members rejoice with him. 27 You are the body of Christ and you are its members, each in your own way. 28 God has appointed in the Church first of all apostles, second prophets, third teachers, then those with gifts of miracles, healing, assistance, governance, and speaking in various tongues. 29 Are they all apostles? All prophets? All teachers? 30 Are they all miracle workers? Do they all possess the grace of healing? Do they all speak in tongues? Do they all interpret? 31 Aspire to the highest forms of charisma. I will show you a path that is superior to all others.

          1 Corinthians 13

          1 If I speak in the tongues of men and of angels, but do not have love, I am only a resounding gong or a clanging cymbal. 2 If I have the gift of prophecy, and understand all mysteries, and possess all knowledge, and if I have all faith, so that I can remove mountains, but do not have love, I am nothing. 3 If I give away all my possessions to feed the poor, if I give my body to be burned, but do not have charity, it all profits me nothing. 4 Love is patient, love is kind.           1 Corinthians 14

          1 Pursue charity. Nevertheless, aspire to spiritual gifts, especially that of prophecy. 2 Indeed, the one who speaks in a tongue does not speak to men, but to God; for no one understands him, and it is in the spirit that he speaks mysteries. 3 On the contrary, the one who prophesies speaks to men, edifies them, exhorts them, and comforts them. 4 He who speaks in a tongue edifies himself, but he who prophesies edifies the church [of God]. 5 I want you all to speak in tongues, but even more to prophesy, because the one who prophesies is greater than the one who speaks in tongues, unless the latter interprets what he says, so that the church may be edified. 6 Come now, brothers, what good would it be to you if I came to you speaking in tongues and did not speak to you by revelation, or by knowledge, or by prophecy, or by teaching? 7 If inanimate objects that produce sound, such as a flute or a harp, do not produce distinct sounds, how will we know what is being played on the flute or the harp? 8 And if the trumpet makes a confused sound, who will prepare for battle? 9 Likewise, if you do not speak clearly with your tongue, how will anyone know what you are saying? You will be speaking into the air. 10 However numerous the various languages may be in the world, there is none that consists of unintelligible sounds. 11 If I am ignorant of the value of sound, I will be a barbarian to the speaker, and the speaker will be a barbarian to me. 12 Likewise, since you desire spiritual gifts, strive to excel in them for the building up of the church. 13 Therefore, let the one who speaks in a tongue pray for the gift of interpretation. 14 For if I pray in a tongue, my spirit prays, but my mind remains unfruitful. 15 What then should I do? I will pray with the spirit, but I will also pray with the understanding; I will sing with the spirit, but I will also sing with the understanding. 16 Otherwise, if you give thanks with the spirit, how will someone who is among the common people respond "Amen" to your thanksgiving, since he does not know what you are saying? 17 Your hymn of thanksgiving is undoubtedly very beautiful, but he is not edified by it. 18I thank my God that I speak in tongues more than all of you, 19 But in the church, I would rather speak five words with my understanding, so as to instruct others also, than ten thousand words in a tongue. 20 Brothers, do not be children in your thinking, but be children in your malice, and in your thinking be mature. 21 It is written in the Law: «With men of a different tongue and through foreign lips I will speak to this people, and even then they will not listen to me, says the Lord.» 22 Therefore, tongues are a sign, not for believers, but for disbelievers; prophecy, on the contrary, is a sign, not for disbelievers, but for believers. 23 Therefore, if the whole church comes together and everyone speaks in tongues, and some uninitiated or unbelievers come in, will they not say that you are crazy? 24 But if everyone prophesies and an unbeliever or an uninitiated man appears, he is convicted by everyone, he is judged by everyone., 25 the secrets of his heart are revealed, so that, falling face down to the ground, he will worship God and proclaim that God is truly in your midst. 26 What then shall we do, my brothers? When you come together, one of you has a hymn, another a teaching, another a revelation, another a speech in tongues, another an interpretation; all these things are done to build up. 27 If we speak in a language, let it be two or three at most at a time, each taking their turn, and let there be an interpreter, 28 If there is no interpreter, let us be silent in the assembly and speak to ourselves and to God. 29 As for the prophets, let two or three speak and let the others judge, 30 And if another, who is sitting, has a revelation, let the first one remain silent. 31 For you can all prophesy one after another, so that all may be instructed and all may be exhorted. 32 But the spirits of the prophets are subject to the prophets. 33 because God is not a God of disorder, but of peace. 34 As has been the case in all the churches of the saints, let your women remain silent in the assemblies, for they are not commissioned to speak, but let them be submissive, as the Law also says. 35 If they want to learn about something, let them ask their husbands at home, for it is not proper for a woman to speak in the assembly. 36 Did the word of God originate from you? Or have you alone received it? 37 If anyone thinks he is a prophet or rich in spiritual gifts, let him acknowledge that these things which I have written to you are the commandments of the Lord. 38 And if he wants to ignore it, let him ignore it. 39 Therefore, my brothers, earnestly desire the gift of prophecy and do not forbid speaking in tongues. 40 But let everything be done with decorum and order.

          1 Corinthians 15

          1 I remind you, brothers, of the gospel I preached to you, which you received and in which you have stood firm. 2 and by which you are also saved, if you hold fast to the word I preached to you, unless you have believed in vain. 3 I taught you first of all what I myself learned: that Christ died for our sins in accordance with the Scriptures, 4 that he was buried and that he was raised on the third day, in accordance with the Scriptures, 5 and that he appeared to Cephas, then to the Twelve. 6After that, he appeared all at once to more than five hundred brothers, most of whom are still alive and some of whom have fallen asleep. 7 Then he appeared to James, and then to all the apostles. 8 After all of them, he also appeared to me, as to the abortion. 9 For I am the least of the apostles, unworthy to be called an apostle, because I persecuted the church of God. 10 It is by the grace of God that I am what I am, and his grace toward me was not in vain, far from it. I worked harder than all of them—yet not I, but the grace of God that is with me. 11 So then, whether it be I or they, this is what we preach, and this is what you have believed. 12 Now if Christ is preached as having been raised from the dead, how can some of you say that there is no resurrection of the dead? 13 If there is no resurrection of the dead, then Christ has not been resurrected either. 14 And if Christ has not been raised, our preaching is useless and so is your faith. 15 It turns out that we are false witnesses about God, since we have testified against him that he raised Christ from the dead, whereas he would not have raised him if it is true that the dead do not rise. 16 For if the dead are not raised, then Christ has not been raised either. 17 And if Christ has not been raised, your faith is futile; you are still in your sins., 18 Those who have fallen asleep in Christ are lost. 19 If we have hope in Christ only for this life, we are the most miserable of all men. 20 But now Christ has been raised from the dead, he is the firstfruits of those who have fallen asleep. 21 For since death came through a man, the resurrection of the dead also comes through a man. 22 For as in Adam all die, so in Christ all will be made alive., 23 but each in his own order, Christ as the firstfruits, then those who belong to Christ at his coming. 24 Then it will be the end, when he hands over the kingdom to God and the Father, after having destroyed every principality, every power, and every force. 25 For he must reign "until he has put all his enemies under his feet."« 26 The last enemy to be destroyed is death. 27 For God «put all things under his feet.» But when Scripture says that all things were put under him, it is evident that this one is excepted, who put all things under him. 28 And when all things have been put under him, then the Son himself will pay homage to him who put all things under him, so that God may be all in all. 29 Otherwise, what would those who are baptized for the dead be doing? If the dead do not rise in any way, why are they baptized for them? 30 And why are we ourselves in danger at every hour? 31 Every day I face death, as surely, my brothers, as you are my glory in Jesus Christ our Lord. 32If I fought against the beasts at Ephesus with purely human aims, what do I gain? If the dead do not rise, «Let us eat and drink, for tomorrow we die.» 33 Don't be seduced by the saying, "Bad company corrupts good morals."« 34 Come to your senses and stop sinning, for there are some who are ignorant of God—I say this to your shame. 35 But someone will ask, “How are the dead raised? With what kind of body will they come?” 36 Fool, what you sow will not come back to life unless it dies first. 37 And what you sow is not the body that will one day be, but a single grain, whether of wheat or some other seed., 38 But God gives it a body as he willed, and to each seed he gives the body that is proper to it. 39 Not all flesh is the same flesh; the flesh of men is one thing, that of quadrupeds another, that of birds another, that of fish another. 40 There are also celestial bodies and terrestrial bodies, but the brilliance of celestial bodies is of a different nature than that of terrestrial bodies: 41 The brightness of the sun is one thing, the brightness of the moon is another, and the brightness of the stars is another; even one star differs in brightness from another star. 42 So it is with the resurrection of the dead. Though sown in corruption, the body is raised incorruptible., 43 Sown in shame, it is raised in glory; sown in weakness, it is raised in power., 44It is sown in an animal body, it is raised in a spiritual body. If there is an animal body, there is also a spiritual body. 45 It is in this sense that it is written: "The first man, Adam, was made a living soul," the last Adam was made a life-giving spirit. 46 But it was not the spiritual that was made first, it was the animal, and the spiritual came after. 47 The first man, taken from the earth, is earthly; the second comes from heaven. 48 As is the earthly, so are the earthly; and as is the heavenly, so are the heavenly. 49 And just as we have borne the image of the earthly, we shall also bear the image of the heavenly. 50 What I am saying, brothers, is that neither flesh nor blood can inherit the kingdom of God, and that corruption will not inherit incorruptibility. 51 Here is a mystery I reveal to you: We will not all fall asleep, but we will all be changed., 52 in an instant, in the twinkling of an eye, at the sound of the last trumpet, for the trumpet will sound and the dead will be raised incorruptible, and we will be changed. 53 For this corruptible body must put on incorruptibility, and this mortal body must put on immortality. 54 When this corruptible body has put on incorruptibility, and this mortal body has put on immortality, then the saying that is written will be fulfilled: «Death has been swallowed up in victory.» 55 «"O death, where is thy victory? O death, where is thy sting?"» 56 Now the sting of death is sin, and the power of sin is the law. 57But thanks be to God, who has given us the victory through our Lord Jesus Christ. 58 Therefore, my beloved brothers, be steadfast, immovable, always abounding in the work of the Lord, knowing that in the Lord your labor is not in vain.

            1 As for the collection for the saints, you also must follow the instructions I gave to the churches of Galatia. 2 On the first day of the week, each of you should set aside at home and gather what you can save, so that collections will not have to be made until my arrival. 3And when I arrive, I will send with letters those whom you have designated, to carry your gifts to Jerusalem. 4 If it is appropriate for me to go myself, they will make the trip with me. 5 I will come to your house after I have passed through Macedonia, for I will only be passing through it, 6 but perhaps I will stay with you, or even spend the winter there, so that you may accompany me where I have to go. 7 I do not want to see you only in passing this time, but I hope to stay with you for some time, if the Lord permits. 8 However, I will remain in Ephesus until Pentecost., 9 because a door is open to me, wide and effective, and the adversaries are numerous. 10 If Timothy comes to you, see that he is not afraid among you, for he is doing the work of the Lord, just as I am. 11 Therefore, let no one despise him. You know that the family of Stephanas was the first convert in Achaia and has devoted itself to the service of the saints: 16 In turn, show deference to men of this merit and to anyone who cooperates and works on the same task. 17 I am pleased by the presence of Stephanas, Fortunat, and Achaique; they have made up for your absence., 18 because they have calmed my mind and yours.
