Hebrews 1

1 Having spoken to our ancestors through the Prophets at many times and in various ways, God, 2 in these last days he has spoken to us by his Son, whom he appointed heir of all things and through whom also he made the world. 3 This Son, who is the radiance of his glory, the exact representation of his being, and who sustains all things by his powerful word, after having purified us from our sins, sat down at the right hand of the Majesty in the highest heaven, 4 greater than the angels, because the name he possesses is more excellent than theirs. 5 To which of the angels did God ever say, "You are my Son, today I have begotten you"? Or again, "I will be his Father and he will be my Son"? 6 And when he again brings the Firstborn into the world, he says, «Let all God’s angels worship him.» 7 Moreover, while it is said of the angels: "He who makes of his angels winds and of his servants a flame of fire,"« 8 He said to the Son: «Your throne, O God, is forever and ever; the scepter of your kingdom is a scepter of righteousness. 9 You have loved righteousness and hated wickedness; therefore God, your God, has anointed you with the oil of gladness above all your companions.» 10 And again: «It is you, Lord, who in the beginning laid the foundations of the earth, and the heavens are the work of your hands, 11 They will perish, but you remain; they will all wear out like a garment., 12 Like a coat you will roll them up and they will be changed, but you remain the same and your years will not end.» 13 And to which of the angels did he ever say, "Sit at my right hand, until I make your enemies a footstool for your feet"? 14 Are they not all spirits serving God, sent as servants for the good of those who are to receive the inheritance of salvation?

Hebrews 2

1 That is why we must pay closer attention to the things we have heard, lest we be led astray. 2 For if the word spoken by angels has already had its effect, and if every transgression and disobedience has received in return a just punishment, 3 How can we escape, if we were to neglect such a salutary message, which, first announced by the Lord, has surely been transmitted to us by those who heard it from him?, 4 God confirming their testimony by signs, wonders, and all kinds of miracles, as well as by gifts of the Holy Spirit, distributed according to his will? 5 Indeed, it is not to angels that God has subjected the world to come, of which we are speaking. 6 Someone also wrote this testimony somewhere: «What is man that you are mindful of him, or the son of man that you care for him?” 7 You made him for a little while lower than the angels, you crowned him with glory and honor, [you established him over the works of your hands], 8 You have put everything under his feet.» Indeed, in subjecting everything to him, God left nothing that is not subject to him. Yet at present we do not see everything subject to him. 9 But we see Jesus, who "was made lower than the angels for a little while," crowned with glory and honor because of the death he suffered, so that by the grace of God he might taste death for everyone. 10 Indeed, it was truly fitting for him for whom and through whom all things exist, that, having to lead a great number of sons to glory, he should raise up through sufferings to the highest degree of perfection the head who guided them to salvation. 11 For he who sanctifies and those who are sanctified are all of one. That is why Jesus Christ is not ashamed to call them brothers, 12 when he said, «I will declare your name to my brothers, I will praise you in the midst of the assembly.» 13 And again: «I will put my trust in him.» And again: «Here I am, and the children God has given me.» 14 Since the children shared in flesh and blood, he too shared in the same, so that by his death he might break the power of him who has the dominion of death, that is, the devil., 15 and to deliver those whom the fear of death had kept all their lives subject to servitude. 16 For indeed it is not angels that he comes to the aid of, but the posterity of Abraham. 17 Therefore, he had to be made like his brothers in every way, so that he might be a merciful High Priest and faithfully fulfill what was required before God to atone for the sins of the people., 18 For it is because he himself suffered and was afflicted that he is able to help those who are afflicted.

    Hebrews 3

    1 Therefore, holy brothers and sisters, who share in the heavenly calling, fix your thoughts on Jesus, the apostle and high priest of our professed faith., 2 who is faithful to the one who established him, just as Moses was "faithful in all his house."« 3 For he surpasses Moses in dignity, just as the one who built a house has more honor than the house itself. 4 For every house is built by someone, and the one who built everything is God. 5 While Moses was "faithful in all God's house," as a servant, to bear witness to what he had to say, 6 Christ was faithful as a son, as head of his own house, and his house is us, provided we hold fast to the end the open profession of our faith and the hope which is our glory. 7 That is why, as the Holy Spirit says: «Today, if you hear his voice, 8 Do not harden your hearts, as happened at the place called Contradiction, on the day of testing in the desert, 9 where your fathers challenged me to test me, however, they had seen my works for forty years. 10 Therefore I was angry with that generation and said, “Their hearts continually go astray; they have not known my ways.”. 11 So I swore in my anger: They shall not enter my rest.» 12 Take care, my brothers, that none of you has an evil and faithless heart, which causes him to abandon the living God. 13 On the contrary, exhort one another daily, as long as this time called «Today» lasts, so that none of you may be hardened, deceived by sin. 14 For we have entered into Christ’s participation, provided that we hold fast to the end the beginning of our being in him, 15 While we are also told: "Today, if you hear his voice, do not harden your hearts, as in the place called Contradiction."« 16 Who, indeed, were those who, after "hearing the voice of God," rebelled? But were they not all those who had come out of Egypt under the leadership of Moses? 17 And against whom was God "angry for forty years"? Was it not against those who had sinned, whose corpses were scattered in the desert? 18 «"And to whom did he swear that they would not enter his rest," if not to those who had disobeyed? 19 Indeed, we see that they were unable to enter because of their disobedience.

      Hebrews 4

      1 Therefore, let us be afraid, while the promise "to enter into his rest" is still in effect, that none of you should come to be frustrated. 2 For the joyful message was addressed to us as well as to them, but the message which was spoken to them did not profit them, because it was not allied with faith among those who heard it. 3 On the contrary, we who believe will enter that rest, just as he said: «I swore in my anger, »They shall not enter my rest.’” He says this, even though his works have been finished since the beginning of the world. 4 For it is said somewhere concerning the seventh day: "And God rested from all his works on the seventh day."« 5 And here again: "They shall not enter my rest."« 6 Since some must enter, and those who first received the promise did not enter because of their disobedience, 7 God again sets a day which he calls "today", saying in David so long after, as we saw above: "Today, if you hear his voice, do not harden your hearts."« 8 For if Joshua had brought them into "rest," David would not have spoken after that of another day. 9 So there remains one day of rest reserved for the people of God. 10 Indeed, he who enters "into God's rest" also rests from his works, just as God rested from his. 11 Let us therefore hasten to enter into that rest, so that no one may fall by following the same example of disobedience. 12 For the word of God is living and active, sharper than any double-edged sword, it penetrates even to dividing soul and spirit, joints and marrow, and discerning the thoughts and attitudes of the heart. 13 Therefore, nothing in all creation is hidden from God’s sight, but everything is naked and exposed to the eyes of him to whom we must give account. 14 Therefore, since we have in Jesus, the Son of God, an excellent high priest who has entered the heavens, let us hold fast our confession of faith. 15 For we do not have a high priest who is powerless to sympathize with our weaknesses, to be like us, but who has experienced all our weaknesses except sin. 16 Let us then approach God’s throne of grace with confidence, so that we may receive mercy and find grace to help us in our time of need.

      Hebrews 5

      1 Indeed, every high priest is chosen from among men to act on behalf of men in matters of worship of God, to offer oblations and sacrifices for sins. 2 He is able to show leniency towards those who sin through ignorance and error, since he himself is surrounded by weakness. 3 And it is because of this weakness that he must offer sacrifices for sins, both for himself and for the people. 4 And no one arrogates this dignity to themselves; one must be called to it by God, like Aaron. 5Thus Christ did not rise to the glory of the supreme pontificate of himself, but received it from the one who said to him: «You are my Son, today I have begotten you,», 6 As he also says in another place: "You are a priest forever, in the order of Melchizedek."« 7 It was he who, during the days of his flesh, offered up prayers and supplications with loud cries and tears to him who was able to save him from death, and was heard because of his piety, 8 He learned, as a Son though he was, through his own sufferings, what it means to obey, 9 And now that it has reached its end, it saves forever all those who obey it. 10 God having declared him "high priest according to the order of Melchizedek."« 11 On this subject, we would have a lot to say and things difficult to explain to you, because you have become slow to understand. 12 For though by this time you ought to be teachers, you need someone to teach you the basic principles of God’s word all over again. You need milk instead of solid food. 13 Anyone who is still on milk is not capable of perfect speech because he is a child. 14 But solid food is for mature men, for those whose senses are trained by habit to distinguish good from evil.

        Hebrews 6

        1 Therefore, leaving aside the elementary teaching about Christ, let us move on to the perfect teaching, without laying down again the fundamental principles of renunciation of dead works, of faith in God, 2 of the doctrine of ablutions, the laying on of hands, the resurrection of the dead and eternal judgment. 3 That's what we're going to do, God willing. 4 For it is impossible for those who have once been enlightened, who have tasted the heavenly gift, who have shared in the Holy Spirit, 5 who have tasted the sweetness of God's word and the wonders of the world to come, 6 and who have fallen, to renew them a second time by bringing them to repentance, they who for their part crucify the Son of God again and deliver him to ignominy. 7 When land, nourished by the rain that often falls upon it, produces grass useful to those for whom it is cultivated, it shares in God's blessing. 8 but if it produces only thorns and thistles, it is judged to be of poor quality, close to being cursed and eventually it is set on fire. 9 However, beloved, although we speak thus, we have a better opinion of you, one more favorable to your salvation. 10 For God is not unjust to forget your works and the love you have shown for his name, you who have ministered to the saints and continue to minister to them. 11 We desire that each of you show the same zeal to the end, so that your hopes may be fulfilled, 12 so that you do not become sluggish, but imitate those who through faith and perseverance enter into the promised inheritance. 13 In the promise he made to Abraham, since God could not swear by anyone greater than himself, he swore by himself., 14 and said, «Yes, I will bless you and multiply you.» 15 And so it was that this patriarch, having waited patiently, came into possession of the promise. 16 Indeed, men swear by one who is greater than themselves, and the oath, serving as a guarantee, puts an end to all their disputes. 17 Therefore, God, wishing to demonstrate more clearly to the heirs of the promise the immutable stability of his plan, introduced the oath, 18 so that, by two unchangeable things, in which it is impossible for God to deceive us, we who have sought refuge in him may be strongly encouraged to hold fast to the hope set before us. 19 We hold it like an anchor of the soul, sure and firm, this hope that penetrates even beyond the veil, 20 into the sanctuary where Jesus entered as our forerunner, as "high priest forever, according to the order of Melchizedek."«

          Hebrews 7

          1 This Melchizedek, king of Salem, priest of the Most High God, who came to meet Abraham upon his return from the defeat of the kings, blessed him, 2 and to whom Abraham gave a tenth of all the spoils, who is first, according to the meaning of his name, king of righteousness, then king of Salem, that is, king of peace, 3 who is without father, without mother, without genealogy, who has neither beginning of days nor end of life, and who has thus become like the Son of God: this Melchizedek remains a priest forever. 4 Consider how great is the one to whom Abraham, the patriarch, gave a tithe of the best of what he had. 5 Those of the sons of Levi who obtain the priesthood are commanded, according to the Law, to collect tithes from the people, that is, from their brothers, who nevertheless also came from the blood of Abraham., 6 And he, who was not of their race, collected a tithe from Abraham and blessed him who had the promises. 7 Now without dispute, the lesser is blessed by the greater. 8 Furthermore, here those who collect tithes are men who die, but there it is a man who is attested to be alive. 9 And Levi himself, who receives the tithe, paid it, so to speak, in the person of Abraham., 10 for he was still in his ancestor when Melchizedek went to meet him. 11 If perfection could have been achieved by the Levitical priesthood, since it was under it that the people received the law, what need was there for another priest to arise "according to the order of Melchizedek" and not according to the order of Aaron? 12 For, since the priesthood has been changed, it is necessary that the Law also be changed. 13 Indeed, the one of whom these words are spoken belongs to another tribe, none of whose members served at the altar: 14 It is well known that our Lord came from Judah, a tribe to which Moses never attributed the priesthood. 15 This becomes even more evident if another priest arises in the likeness of Melchizedek, 16 instituted, not according to the prescriptions of a carnal law, but according to the power of a life that does not end, 17 According to this testimony: "You are a priest forever, in the order of Melchizedek."« 18 Thus, the first ordinance was repealed because of its impotence and uselessness., 19 For the law made nothing perfect, but it was a gateway to a better hope, through which we have access to God. 20 And since this was not done without an oath, for while the others were appointed priests without an oath, 21 This was done with an oath by the one who said to him, «The Lord has sworn and will not change his mind: You are a priest forever, in the order of Melchizedek.», 22 Jesus is therefore the guarantor of a higher covenant. 23 Moreover, they themselves form a long line of priests, because death prevented them from remaining so indefinitely., 24 But he, because he remains eternally, possesses a priesthood that is not passed on. 25 This is also why he is able to save completely those who come to God through him, since he always lives to intercede for them. 26 Such, indeed, is the high priest we needed: holy, innocent, spotless, separated from sinners and exalted above the heavens, 27 who does not need, like the chief priests, to offer sacrifices every day, first for his own sins, and then for those of the people, for this he did once for all when he offered himself. 28 For the Law appoints as high priests men who are subject to weakness, but the word of the oath, which came after the Law, appoints the Son who has been made perfect for eternity.

            Hebrews 8

            1 That being said, the essential point is that we now have a high priest who has sat down at the right hand of the throne of divine majesty in heaven, 2 as minister of the sanctuary and of the true tabernacle, which was set up by the Lord and not by a man. 3 For every high priest is appointed to offer oblations and sacrifices, therefore it is necessary that he too should have something to offer. 4 If he were on earth, he would not even be a priest, since there are already priests there charged with offering sacrifices according to the Law., 5 who celebrate a worship that is only an image and a shadow of heavenly things, as Moses was divinely warned when he had to build the tabernacle: «See,» said the Lord, “you shall make everything according to the pattern shown you on the mountain.” 6 But our high priest has received a ministry of a much higher order, as he is the mediator of a higher covenant, which is based on better promises. 7 Indeed, if the first alliance had been without flaw, there would have been no need to substitute a second one. 8 For it is indeed a rebuke that God expresses when he says to them: «Behold, says the Lord, the days are coming when I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel and with the house of Judah, 9 not a covenant like the one I made with their ancestors when I took them by the hand to lead them out of the land of Egypt. Because they did not remain faithful to my covenant, I also abandoned them, says the Lord. 10 But this is the covenant that I will make with the house of Israel after those days, says the Lord: I will put my laws in their minds and write them on their hearts, and I will be their God and they shall be my people. 11 None of them will teach their fellow citizen anymore, nor will they teach their brother, saying, 'Know the Lord,' for they will all know me, from the least of them to the greatest. 12 I will forgive their iniquities and I will remember their sins no more.» 13 By saying, "A new covenant," God declared the first one obsolete; and what has become obsolete, what is outdated, is about to disappear.

              Hebrews 9

              1 The first covenant also had its own regulations regarding worship and an earthly sanctuary. 2 Indeed, a tabernacle was built, with a front part, called the holy place, where were the lampstand, the table, and the bread of the Presence. 3Behind the second veil was the part of the tabernacle called the Holy of Holies, having a golden altar for incense and the ark of the covenant all covered in gold. 4 Inside the ark was a golden urn containing the manna, Aaron's staff which had blossomed, and the tablets of the covenant. 5 Above were cherubim of glory, overshadowing the mercy seat. But this is not the place to speak in detail on this subject. 6 Now, with these things thus arranged, the priests regularly enter the front part of the tabernacle when they perform the service of worship, 7 The high priest alone, only once a year, enters into the second part, but with blood which he offers for himself and for the sins of the people. 8 The Holy Spirit shows by this that the way to the Holy of Holies has not yet been opened, as long as the first tabernacle remains. 9 This is a figure which relates to the present time; it signifies that the oblations and sacrifices offered cannot bring to perfection, from the point of view of conscience, the one who renders this worship. 10 For the prescriptions relating to food, drink and various ablutions are only carnal ordinances, imposed only until a time of reformation. 11 But when Christ appeared as high priest of the good things to come, he did so in a more excellent and perfect tabernacle, not made with human hands, that is to say, not belonging to this creation. 12 And it was not with the blood of goats and bulls, but with his own blood, that he entered once for all into the Holy of Holies, having obtained eternal redemption. 13For if the blood of goats and bulls, and the ashes of a heifer sprinkled on those who are defiled, sanctify so as to purify the flesh, 14 How much more will the blood of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered himself without blemish to God, purify our consciences from dead works to serve the living God? 15 And that is why he is the mediator of a new covenant, so that, since his death took place for the forgiveness of the transgressions committed under the first covenant, those who are called may receive the eternal inheritance that was promised to them. 16 Because, where there is a will, the death of the testator is necessary., 17 because a will only takes effect upon death, being without force while the testator is alive. 18 That is why even the first alliance was not inaugurated without bloodshed. 19 Moses, after proclaiming all the commandments according to the tenor of the Law before all the people, took the blood of bulls and goats, with water, scarlet wool, and hyssop, and sprinkled it on the Book itself and on all the people, saying: 20 «This is the blood of the covenant that God made with you.”. 21 «"He also sprinkled the tabernacle and all the utensils of worship with the blood. 22 And according to the Law, almost everything is purified with blood, and without the shedding of blood there is no remission. 23 Since the images of the things that are in heaven had to be purified in this way, it was therefore necessary that the heavenly things themselves be inaugurated by sacrifices superior to those. 24 For Christ did not enter a sanctuary made with human hands, an image of the true one, but he entered heaven itself, in order to now be present in the presence of God on our behalf. 25 And it is not to offer himself up again and again, as the high priest enters the sanctuary every year with blood that is not his own: 26 Otherwise he would have had to suffer many times since the foundation of the world, but he appeared only once in the last ages to abolish sin by his sacrifice. 27 And since it is decreed that men die only once, after which comes judgment, 28 So Christ, having offered himself once to take away the sins of many, will appear a second time, without sin, to give salvation to those who are waiting for him.

                Hebrews 10

                1 The Law, in fact, having only a shadow of the good things to come and not the very image of things, can never, by these same sacrifices which are offered without interruption each year, perfectly sanctify those who approach it. 2 Otherwise, they would not have ceased to be offered, because those who perform this worship, once purified, would no longer have any awareness of their sins. 3 While these sacrifices serve to remind us each year of sins, 4 because it is impossible for the blood of bulls and goats to take away sins. 5 This is why Christ said, upon entering the world: «Sacrifice and offering you did not desire, but a body you prepared for me, 6 You did not accept burnt offerings or sin offerings. 7 Then I said, »Here I am, for it is written of me in the scroll of the book; I have come, O God, to do your will.” 8 After beginning by saying, «You did not desire nor were pleased with sacrifices, burnt offerings, or sin offerings,» all of which are offered according to the Law, 9 He then adds: "Here I am, I have come to do your will." He thus abolishes the first point, to establish the second. 10 It is by this will that we have been sanctified through the offering of his own body, Jesus Christ, once for all. 11 And while every priest presents himself daily to perform his ministry and offers the same victims repeatedly, which can never take away sins, 12 He, on the contrary, after offering a single sacrifice for sins, "sat down" forever "at the right hand of God."« 13 now waiting "for his enemies to become the stepping stone for his feet"« 14 for, by a single offering, he has procured perfection forever for those who are sanctified. 15 This is also what the Holy Spirit testifies to us, for, after having said: 16 «This is the covenant I will make with them after those days,» the Lord adds: “I will put my laws in their hearts and write them on their minds.” 17 and I will remember their sins and their iniquities no more.» 18 However, where sins are forgiven, there is no longer any question of offering for sin. 19 Therefore, brothers, since we have confidence to enter the sanctuary by the blood of Jesus, 20 through the new and living way, which He inaugurated, for us through the veil, that is to say through His flesh 21 And since we have a high priest established over the house of God, 22 Let us draw near with a sincere heart, in the fullness of faith, with our hearts cleansed from the stains of an evil conscience and our bodies washed in pure water. 23 Let us remain steadfast in the profession of our hope, for he who made the promise is faithful. 24 Let us keep an eye on one another to incite one another to charity and good works. 25 Let us not neglect meeting together, as some are in the habit of doing, but let us encourage one another, and all the more as you see the Day approaching. 26 For if we deliberately keep on sinning after we have received the knowledge of the truth, there no longer remains a sacrifice for sins, 27 All that remains is to await a terrible judgment and the jealous fire that will devour the rebels. 28 Anyone who violates the law of Moses dies without mercy, upon the testimony of two or three witnesses., 29 What more severe punishment do you think someone deserves who has trampled the Son of God underfoot, who has treated as unholy the blood of the covenant by which he was sanctified, and who has insulted the Spirit of grace? 30 For we know him, the one who said, «Vengeance is mine; I will repay,» and again, «The Lord will judge his people.» 31 It is terrifying to fall into the hands of the living God. 32 Remember those early days, when, after being enlightened, you endured a great struggle of suffering, sometimes exposed as if on display to scorn and tribulations, 33 sometimes taking part in the suffering of those who were thus treated. 34 Indeed, you have sympathized with the prisoners and you have gladly accepted the plundering of your possessions, knowing that you have a better wealth which will last forever. 35 So don't give up on your insurance, there's a great reward attached to it. 36 For perseverance is necessary so that, after you have done the will of God, you may receive what is promised. 37 Just a little while longer, a very short time, and "he who is to come will come, he will not delay. 38 And my righteous one will live by faith, but if he shrinks back, my soul will not delight in him.» 39 For us, we are not of those who retreat to their destruction, but of those who keep the faith to save their souls.

                Hebrews 11

                1 But faith is the substance of things hoped for, a conviction of things not seen. 2 It was because they possessed it that the ancients obtained a good testimony. 3 It is by faith that we understand that the universe was formed at God’s command, so that the things which are seen were not made out of things which were visible. 4 It was by faith that Abel offered to God a more excellent sacrifice than that of Cain; it was by faith that he was declared righteous, God approving his offerings; and it is by faith that, though dead, he still speaks. 5 It was by faith that Enoch was taken up without having suffered death: "he was not found, because God had taken him away" for before this taking up he had received this testimony that he had pleased God. 6 But without faith it is impossible to please God, for whoever would draw near to God must believe that he exists and that he rewards those who seek him. 7 It was by faith that Noah, being warned by God of things not yet seen, built with holy fear an ark to save his family; it was by faith that he condemned the world and became heir of the righteousness which is obtained by faith. 8 It was by faith that Abraham, obeying God’s call, set out for a land he was to receive as an inheritance, and he went on his journey without knowing where he was going. 9 It was by faith that he sojourned in the promised land, as in a foreign land, dwelling in tents, as did Isaac and Jacob, heirs with him of the same promise. 10 For he was waiting for the city with firm foundations, whose architect and builder is God. 11 It was by faith that Sarah, who was past the age of conception, received the power, because she believed in the faithfulness of Him who had made the promise. 12 That is why, from one man, already as if dead, came forth a posterity as numerous as the stars in the sky and the countless grains of sand that are on the seashore. 13 It was in faith that these patriarchs all died, not having received the effect of the promises, but having seen him and greeted him from afar, confessing "that they were strangers and exiles on the earth."« 14 Those who speak in this way clearly show that they are looking for a homeland. 15 And certainly, if they had meant by that the one from which they had come, they would have had the means to return there. 16 But it is to a better homeland, to the heavenly homeland, that their aspirations are directed. That is why God is not ashamed to call himself "their God," for he has prepared a city for them. 17 It was by faith that Abraham, being tested, offered Isaac as a sacrifice. 18 Thus he who had received the promises and to whom it had been said, «Through Isaac your offspring will be born,» offered this only son, 19 Believing that God is powerful enough to resurrect even the dead, he also found him as if in a figure. 20It was by faith that Isaac blessed Jacob and Esau, in view of things to come. 21 By faith Jacob, as he was dying, blessed each of Joseph's sons and bowed down, leaning on the top of his scepter. 22 By faith Joseph, near the end of his life, spoke about the exodus of the children of Israel and gave instructions concerning those left behind. 23 It was by faith that Moses, after his birth, was hidden for three months by his parents, because they saw that the child was beautiful and they were not afraid of the king’s edict. 24 It was by faith that Moses, when he had grown up, renounced the title of son of Pharaoh's daughter., 25 preferring to be mistreated with God's people than to enjoy the fleeting pleasure found in sin, 26 He considered the reproach of Christ as a greater wealth than the treasures of Egypt, for he had his eyes fixed on the reward. 27 It was by faith that he left Egypt, not fearing the king’s anger, for he persevered, as though he saw him who is invisible. 28 By faith he kept the Passover and sprinkled the blood, so that the one who destroys the firstborn would not touch the firstborn of Israel. 29 It was by faith that they crossed the Red Sea as if it were dry land, while the Egyptians who attempted the crossing were swallowed up. 30 It was by faith that the walls of Jericho fell down, after they had been encircled for seven days. 31 It was by faith that Rahab the courtesan did not perish with the rebels, for having given the spies safe hospitality. 32 And what else shall I say? I would run out of time if I wanted to speak also of Gideon, Barak, Samson, Jephthah, David, Samuel and the prophets. 33 Through faith they conquered kingdoms, administered justice, obtained the fulfillment of the promises, shut the mouths of lions, 34 extinguished the violence of fire, escaped the edge of the sword, triumphed over disease, displayed their valor in war, put enemy armies to flight, 35 Through them, women recovered their resurrected dead.
